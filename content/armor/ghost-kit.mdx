The Ghost Kit is a [Leather Kit](/armor/war-steel-doctrine#the-three-armor-kits) of black-dyed leather, worn by the [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard) on their deployed missions — assassinations, inquisitions, and the operations the Empire never sees. Near-black rune-imbued leather with dark gold scrollwork, over dark purple cloth that reads as black in low light, it carries full magical capability identical to [War Plate](/armor/steel-war-plate), rendered invisible rather than diminished.

The Purple Guard render the already-silent leather absolute through sound-suppression magic: silencing footsteps, dampening breath, nullifying the twang of a bowstring and the whisper of a sword clearing its scabbard. An enemy never hears a Purple Guard in the Ghost Kit. Combined with atmospheric sound-suppression drawn from [Ranger doctrine](/grand-army/green-rangers) and elevated to Guard precision, it produces zero meaningful visual or auditory signature.

## The Ranger Kit Variant

A rarer configuration, informally called the Ranger Kit, dresses a Guard in brown rune-imbued leather over purple cloth with gold filigree — reserved for the exceptional case of Purple Guard deployment embedded within a Ranger battalion. It trades the Ghost Kit's total anonymity for something narrower: identifiable to allies, invisible to none of them. See [the Ranger Leather Kit](/armor/ranger-leather-kit) for the base pattern it borrows from.

## Deployment Doctrine

Ghost Missions — assassination, intelligence extraction, target elimination — are conducted in the Ghost Kit with zero signature and no witnesses. [Inquisition operations](/grand-army/purple-guard#duties-doctrine) may run in War Plate, Light Kit, or Ghost Kit as the situation requires. The Ghost Kit is what a soldier wears when the mission's success is measured by whether anyone ever learns it happened.

## Notable Deployments

The Ghost Kit is the Guard's signature in two of the Empire's most closely held records:

- **[The Purple Hour](/history/the-purple-hour)** — the Phoenix Commander entered Emberhall's palace in Ghost Kit, unfought, and ended the Terran alliance's southern flank in a single detonation of the Phoenix Cloak.
- **[The Night of the Silent Knock](/history/the-night-of-the-silent-knock)** — six Inquisitors in Ghost Kit crossed an entire estate's wards and guards without a sound; the knock at the door was the first noise they made.

> Archivist's note: this record is filed at Restricted clearance consistent with the Guard's own doctrine — the Ghost Kit's purpose is defeated by the degree to which it is documented. What is certified above is limited to composition and confirmed historical deployment.
