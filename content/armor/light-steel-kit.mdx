The Light Kit is the standard armor worn by most soldiers across the [Grand Army](/grand-army) whenever they are not on the front lines — the configuration a soldier is in more often than any other, across every division, on any given day the Army is not actively fighting.

The Light Steel Kit is the Army's configuration for everything short of the front line, issued to all units as the standard alternative to the [Steel War Plate](/armor/steel-war-plate). It retains a rigid steel core — breastplate, armguards, pauldrons, and shin guards — while replacing the heavier plate everywhere else with cloth and leather, and it is issued with a hooded cloak in the division's color that provides camouflage, weather protection, and rank identification at once. Protection is kept where it matters most; weight and rigidity are shed everywhere else.

## Composition

Polished war-steel breastplate, often embossed with floral or wind motifs, with segmented war-steel spaulders allowing full arm articulation for archery, climbing, and close-quarters movement. The mobility gain comes from reduced coverage area, not reduced material quality — the breastplate, spaulders, and vambraces are full-grade war-steel, identically inscribed to the [War Plate](/armor/steel-war-plate), while the scale mail sleeves, thigh tassets, and helmet are omitted in favor of cloth and the hooded cloak. Full steel where it protects; bare mobility everywhere it counts.

## Service

The configuration worn while scouting, while mounted (except by front-line heavy cavalry, who ride in the full War Plate), on garrison duty, on patrol, and while serving in a naval or ranger attachment. Wherever the Army operates but does not expect a pitched battle, the Light Steel Kit is the working uniform.

Cavalry mounts themselves are never armored to match — barding remains leather and rune-scripted rather than plate, so the horse is never burdened or worn down to the rider's protection tier, even when the rider is in full War Plate. See [the Imperial Cavalry](/grand-army/imperial-cavalry) for mounted doctrine.

## Every Division in Light Steel

As with the War Plate, the Light Kit is a shared template dressed in each division's own cloth: hooded in [Ranger green](/grand-army/green-rangers), [Vanguard red](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard), [Cobalt blue](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy), or the [Purple Guard's](/grand-army/purple-guard) own domestic Inquisitor configuration. Two purpose-built descendants of this kit — the [Cobalt Ranger Kit](/armor/cobalt-ranger-kit) and the [Ranger Leather Kit](/armor/ranger-leather-kit) — take the Light Kit's mobility principle further still, trading the steel core for rune-engraved leather. See each division's entry under [the Grand Army](/grand-army) for its colored Light Kit photography.
