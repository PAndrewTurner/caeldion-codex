Weapons policy in the [Grand Army](/grand-army) reflects the Army's broader meritocratic philosophy: standardize only where standardization serves doctrine, and allow personal choice where personal mastery produces better outcomes.

## The Imperial Sword

All three [armor kits](/armor/war-steel-doctrine) are built around the standard imperial sword, which every soldier carries regardless of division or specialization. It is the one weapon the Army does not leave to preference — a common last resort and a common symbol, carried by the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) private and the [Sovereign](/icons/the-sovereign) alike.

## Issue by Kit

The two lighter kits are issued more broadly than the [War Plate](/armor/steel-war-plate): both the [Light Steel Kit](/armor/light-steel-kit) and the [Leather Kit](/armor/war-steel-doctrine#the-three-armor-kits) are issued with daggers, bows, and quivers in addition to the sword, reflecting the scouting, skirmishing, ranged, and stealth roles for which those kits are designed. A soldier's kit announces their footing before a word is spoken — full plate for the line and the court, light steel for the road and the patrol, leather for the missions that are never meant to be seen.

## The Iron Legion — Standardized Arms

The [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) is the only division with mandated weapons. This is consistent with their role as the Army's foundation — uniformity in the line produces uniformity in formation, and formation is what keeps the Legion alive.

| Configuration | Arms |
| --- | --- |
| Heavy Plate | Spear, Shield, Sword |
| Light Kit | Sword, Bow, Quiver |

The heraldic shield carried by Heavy Plate Legionnaires is a deliberate statement: the Iron Legion carries the Sovereign's standard into every engagement. They are not merely fighters — they are the visible declaration of the Sovereign's reach.

## Specialist Divisions — Personal Choice

Every division above the Iron Legion selects its own primary weapon within reasonable doctrinal bounds. This policy exists because the Army has already identified these soldiers' specific talents and placed them in divisions that match those talents — it follows logically that the soldier who thinks in axes should fight with axes, and the soldier who thinks in spears should fight with spears. The result is that veteran specialists develop deeply personal fighting styles built around their chosen weapon over careers spanning decades, which makes [Cobalt Maestro](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) duels particularly unpredictable — a challenger never knows what is coming.

| Division | Primary Weapon |
| --- | --- |
| [Crimson Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) | Runic war hammers, mauls, two-handed swords, battle axes, or any heavy weapon suited to shock assault and siege. Augmented members carry runic variants; Paragons carry Masterwork Steel. |
| [Green Ranger Corps](/grand-army/green-rangers) | Runic or composite bow as primary (mission-mandatory); secondary chosen personally, most carrying both a sword and a dagger. |
| [Engineering Corps](/grand-army/engineering-corps) | Runic battle axes — weapons that function as both combat instruments and structural implements. The axe is the tool; the tool is also the weapon. |
| [Logistics Corps](/grand-army/logistics-corps) | Standard sword for personal defense. The Logistics Corps is not a combat division. |
| [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) | Officers carry runic swords as primary and rely on Voice amplification magic. Maestros carry runic swords, spears, or a personal weapon of choice, plus runic gauntlets for Hybrid Weaving. |
| [Azure Covenant](/grand-army/azure-covenant) | Specialized heavy runic gauntlets as primary. Runic sword carried but drawn only in desperation. |
| [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) | Sword and dagger. No ranged weapons. |
| [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard) | Full Master's Arsenal — runic sword, dagger, spear, bow, and Masterwork runic gauntlets. The only division issued all weapon categories. |

## Ceremonial and Royal Arms

Not every notable weapon in the Army's armory sees the field. The White Fang Spear is the royal armory's ceremonial spear, presented to members of [House Caelaris](/icons/the-royal-family) on completion of Purple Guard combat instruction — a mark of training completed rather than a weapon carried into battle.
