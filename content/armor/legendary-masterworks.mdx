Above the standard Masterwork gear of the divisions sits a rare and dangerous class of artifact: sealed-mana items of the highest order of runic creation. Each stores an immense reserve of mana behind a fixed runic working, so that the wielder need not weave the effect themselves. Pushing mana into the item does not perform the working — it opens the faucet. The item supplies the execution; the wielder must supply the control. This is why such items are not a shortcut to power. A user without the mastery to govern high-level magical output is as likely to be killed by the item as to wield it: without control, the released power can backfire and consume the one holding it. For this reason these weapons are entrusted only to [Maestro](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy)-level combatants or higher — not as a matter of prestige, but of survival.

Because they are Masterwork-grade, these items are extraordinarily difficult to damage or destroy. But destruction — whether by accident or by intent — releases all of their sealed mana at once, in a single catastrophic detonation. For the most powerful examples, that release is enough to level a city quarter. They are, in effect, the superweapons of an individual soldier, and they are guarded, restricted, or stored away accordingly, out of fear of what they can do.

## Origins — Relic and Forging

Such items fall into two categories of origin. The first are ancient relics, some predating the Empire itself, whose making is lost to history — legend attributes them variously to incomparably powerful warriors of the distant past, and rumor, unconfirmed, to the gods themselves. The second are the far rarer modern creations. The Science Division possesses the theoretical knowledge to forge such items, but the cost is staggering: a single creation can demand mana on the order of the combined output of four full army corps. They are not produced at will. Each is a singular object, and each carries a history of its own. Only two modern forgings are confirmed to exist: the [Staff of the Anemoi](#the-staff-of-the-anemoi) and the [Sapphire Circlet](#the-sapphire-circlet).

> Archivist's note: petitioners occasionally confuse this category with the [Sovereign's Amulet](/icons/the-sovereign). The Amulet stands apart from everything catalogued here — it is a Craftsman-made ward of the Science Division, modern, maintained, and reset by the Craftsman after each use, not an ancient relic, and it is treated as a distinct category of Crown equipment rather than as a legendary item in this sense.

## The Staff of the Anemoi

A steel spear, its blade and shaft inlaid with diamonds, sapphires, gold, and silver, with runes running the length of the shaft that glow when the staff is activated. It grants its wielder command over weather and wind — conjuring localized storms, generating lightning, and calling gusts of wind — and the ability to fly or levitate by pushing mana into the spear. Its apex application is to rise into the air, conjure a thunderstorm, and cast lightning down upon those below.

The Staff is one of the rare modern creations, forged by the Empire a century ago in a working that required the combined magical output of one hundred [Azure Covenant](/grand-army/azure-covenant) casters.

### The Frostpeak Theft

Fifty years ago the Staff was stolen, and the thief fled into the Frostpeak Range to the northwest of the [Citadel](/geography/caeldrion). Atop one of the peaks they attempted to wield it. Those within the Citadel saw a flash of light and an explosion of blue electrical energy on the distant summit. The [Black Legionaries](/grand-army/iron-legion) dispatched to the mountaintop found only the spear — undamaged, hovering slightly above the floor of a crater that had not been there before. The thief was never recovered.

## The White Fang Sword

An [Imperial Sword](/armor/sidearms-and-weapons), its blade inlaid with gold and silver and its guard and hilt set with jewels, its silver runes carved into the blade and glowing white when the weapon is drawn to power. It allows the generation of lightning and electrical energy along the blade, and the casting of arcs or bolts of that energy at an enemy. It is the sword worn by, and associated with, [the Sovereign](/icons/the-sovereign) in court — the blade of the throne itself.

## The White Fang Spear

From the White Fang Sword descends a lesser derivative, the White Fang Spear, patterned on and named for the Sovereign's blade. Where the Sword is a legendary artifact wielded by a master, the Spear works on the same push-and-release principle as standard [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) runic arms, at a higher and more dangerous tier: a bearer pushes raw mana into the channel and draws a genuine arc of electrical force from the tip. The White Fang Spears are borne by the [Sanctum Guard](/grand-army/iron-legion#the-full-scope-beyond-the-battlefield), out of history and in recognition of the Sovereign whose weapon they echo.

## The Sapphire Circlet

A gold circlet set with sapphires, granting its wearer command over water and waves. It can conjure and direct localized water, and in the hands of a skilled wielder it can call a wave large enough to wreck an invading fleet — a tsunami turned against a hostile navy. The Circlet is one of the two confirmed modern forgings, made by Caeldrion for the [Sunlit Isles](/geography/the-sunlit-isles) as a gift of good faith when the Isles joined the Empire: a symbol of unity and mutual respect. It is worn by the [King of the Isles](/geography/the-sunlit-isles#governance-the-king-of-the-isles), the leader of that region.

## The Inferno Gauntlet

Not all of these items belong to the Empire. The Inferno Gauntlet is a creation of the Terran Empire — a black armored gauntlet inlaid with glowing red runes that allows the creation of intense flame and jets of fire. It was built out of fear: Terran was terrified of the [Crimson Vanguard's](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) Phoenix Cloak, and set out to forge its own answer to it. It is a lesser item than the Caeldrion-made artifacts, owing to the nature and lesser purity of the metal used in its making — Terran lacks the Science Division's war-steel metallurgy — but it remains a deadly weapon, and a dangerous foe to face on the field.

> Archivist's note: this office catalogues five confirmed items. It does not certify that five is the complete count. An artifact whose making predates the Empire is, by definition, cataloguable only once it surfaces — and this office would be the last to claim it has seen everything the ancient world buried.
