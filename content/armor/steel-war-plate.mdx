The Steel War Plate is the [Grand Army's](/grand-army) heaviest and most complete configuration, issued to every division across all ranks. It is at once the Army's battle armor and its face to the world, carrying a fourfold purpose that applies equally from the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) to the Sovereign's Physician to the [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard):

- **Front-Line Field Combat** — the standard armor for any engagement at the front of a major battle. Maximum protection is the priority; mobility tradeoffs are accepted. Front-line heavy cavalry wear the full War Plate when mounted, rather than the [Light Kit](/armor/light-steel-kit) used by other riders.
- **Dress and Court Uniform** — the formal uniform of the Grand Army for court presentations, award ceremonies, state funerals, and formal reviews. Every division appears in War Plate when representing the Army as an institution rather than a field unit.
- **Diplomatic Configuration** — worn when the Army projects power through presence rather than violence. Gold-Trimmed Masterwork Plate for the [Purple Guard's](/grand-army/purple-guard) diplomatic role; standard War Plate with divisional cloth for all others.

War Plate is mandated whenever a soldier is engaged in frontline combat. Outside of combat, wearing it is always permitted — for court, parade, diplomatic service, or personal preference — and no soldier is barred from it unless specifically ordered otherwise.

## Composition

Maximum-coverage polished war-steel plate, burnished to a mirror-bright finish. Solid breastplate and tassets, full pauldrons, articulated armguards, gauntlets, and greaves, with scale mail integrated throughout the torso and flexible zones. This is the largest surface of inscribed war-steel of any kit, and therefore offers the greatest capacity to manage [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn) through passive ignition — the peak runic performance available to any soldier.

Beneath the plate, an [arming doublet](/arcanum/arming-doublets) of alchemically treated fiber absorbs kinetic shock and heat before it reaches the body — the layer the Archive rarely photographs and every veteran mentions first. It is also where the divisional color lives: the War Plate doublet carries gold filigree trim (silver for the Iron Legion) to match the plate's earned decoration, and each division draws it in its own cloth. ## Helmet & Neck

Ornate but practical open-face helmet with sweeping crest. A coordinating colored cloth neck wrap beneath the helmet is the single most visible rank identifier at close range — an officer or a specialist can be placed by division before their face is seen.

## Distinction — Gold Versus Silver Filigree

All specialist divisions above the Iron Legion feature intricate gold filigree trim; Iron Legion armor carries the same filigree in silver — identical craftsmanship, different metal. The gold filigree is not decorative vanity. It is the Army's visual acknowledgment that this soldier has been evaluated, found exceptional, and invested in. It is earned decoration, not a birthright — see [the Sea of Silver](/grand-army) for the doctrine this enforces.

## War Plate by Preference — the Vanguard and the Maestros

The [Crimson Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) and [Cobalt Maestros](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) in particular tend to choose War Plate more often than the Light Kit even outside front-line combat — not as a requirement, but a preference. The Vanguard favor it for the protection it offers through the exposure that follows the Dump; Maestros favor it for full protection in the heat of battle, made effectively costless by the Weave's mass-negation and amplification effect, which lets War Plate move like a shirt rather than a burden.

## Every Division in Plate

The Steel War Plate is not a single garment but a template: every division below wears the identical configuration in its own cloth and filigree metal, from the [Purple Guard's](/grand-army/purple-guard#issued-war-steel) diamond-sealed plate to the [Imperial Navy's](/grand-army/imperial-navy) marine-piped black. See each division's own entry for its colored plate in full.
