The Ranger Leather Kit is the [Green Rangers'](/grand-army/green-rangers) own configuration of [the Leather Kit](/armor/war-steel-doctrine#the-three-armor-kits) — natural brown hardened leather tooled with floral patterns that mirror the motifs of the standard [war-steel plate](/armor/steel-war-plate), signaling the same institution in a material built for a different purpose entirely. The [Ranger Corps](/grand-army/green-rangers) is the only division fielding three functional armor configurations at once: [War Plate](/armor/steel-war-plate) for full-scale engagements and formal occasions, [Light Kit](/armor/light-steel-kit) with green hooded cloak for standard operations, and this stealth leather variant for deep scouting missions where the mirror-bright surface of war-steel would compromise a position before a single arrow was drawn.

Worn hooded for long-range scouting, hidden sniper positions, and any operation where remaining unseen is the primary tactical requirement — raised for full concealment, lowered when peripheral awareness matters more than total cover.

## Rune System and Doctrine

Like every leather configuration in the Army, the Ranger Leather Kit carries the identical rune system to war-steel plate — the same silver-inlaid inscription circuits, the same passive ignition and heat management described in [runic armor](/arcanum/runic-armor). It hardens to steel density on impact; a Ranger in this kit is not lightly armored, they are differently armored, trading plate's raw coverage for a silhouette an enemy sentry will not notice until it is far too late to matter.

The kit is doctrinally tied to [the Soften](/grand-army/green-rangers#combat-doctrine--the-soften), the Rangers' signature engagement pattern: elimination and disruption from a position the enemy never confirms exists, executed by soldiers this leather was built to make invisible before it was built to protect them.

> Archivist's note: this entry documents the Green Rangers' own leather configuration. For the Purple Guard's black-dyed equivalent, see [the Ghost Kit](/armor/ghost-kit); for the Cobalt Maestros' dark-navy equivalent, see [the Cobalt Ranger Kit](/armor/cobalt-ranger-kit).
