The Grand Army's armor is not silver. It is **war-steel** — a high-grade steel alloy produced by the [Science Division's](/grand-army/logistics-corps) master smiths, into which a measured proportion of silver is worked during smelting. The silver content is not decorative: silver's thermal conductivity and magical receptivity make it the ideal partner for the [runic inscription system](/arcanum/runic-armor), improving the armor's ability to absorb and dissipate the internal heat generated by magical use — [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn).

The resulting alloy is worked, shaped, and burnished to a mirror-bright finish visually indistinguishable from solid silver at any combat distance. The runes themselves are carved directly into the war-steel surface and inlaid with silver — the inlay completes the runic circuit while making the inscription visible as bright lines against the polished steel beneath. The name "the Sea of Silver" is accurate in the way that matters: the Army looks like silver from any angle an enemy or observer would see it. What it actually is, materially, is a more sophisticated and purposeful thing — an alloy engineered specifically for the runic system it carries, produced by the only institution in the Kingdom with the knowledge to make it correctly.

## The Three Armor Kits

While the Sea of Silver ensures visual uniformity, the [Grand Army](/grand-army) is built on three armor kits — the **Steel War Plate**, the **Light Steel Kit**, and the **Leather Kit**. Every other configuration in the Army — the [Ghost Kit](/armor/ghost-kit), the [Cobalt Ranger Kit](/armor/cobalt-ranger-kit), the [Ranger Leather Kit](/armor/ranger-leather-kit), the [Covenant leather](/armor/azure-covenant-armor), the [Shadow Configuration](/grand-army/iron-legion#the-shadow-configuration) — is a subset, variant, or worn-down state of one of these three. The first two are issued to every division; the Leather Kit is issued only to units whose missions require it. One element is constant across all three: every kit incorporates the soldier's divisional color in its cloth, so any member of the Army can be identified at a glance regardless of which kit they wear.

### Kit I — [The Steel War Plate](/armor/steel-war-plate)

The Army's heaviest and most complete configuration, issued across all ranks and divisions. It carries a fourfold purpose: front-line field combat, dress and court uniform, diplomatic configuration, and the default posture whenever the Army appears as an institution rather than a field unit. Full breakdown, including the gold-versus-silver filigree distinction, at its own entry.

### Kit II — [The Light Steel Kit](/armor/light-steel-kit)

The configuration a soldier wears more often than any other — standard issue for scouting, mounted service, garrison duty, and patrol. Retains a rigid steel core at the torso and limbs while shedding plate everywhere else in favor of cloth and a hooded cloak in the wearer's divisional color.

### Kit III — The Leather Kit

Restricted issue: [Green Rangers](/grand-army/green-rangers), Ranger-qualified [Cobalt Maestros](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy), the [Azure Covenant](/grand-army/azure-covenant), and embedded [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) corpsmen. The Leather Kit is not a lesser armor: its rune-engraved leather carries the identical rune system to war-steel plate — the same silver-inlaid inscription circuits, the same passive ignition, the same heat management and amplification described in [runic armor](/arcanum/runic-armor) — and it is enchanted to solidify on impact, supple and silent at rest, hardening to steel density the instant it is struck. A soldier in leather is not less protected or less magically capable than a soldier in plate; they are more mobile. The choice is doctrinal, not a compromise — the large gestures of Covenant bombardment, the precision hand movements of Medical Corps healing, the Maestro's mid-combat Weave, and the silent movement of a ranger or an assassin all demand the freedom leather provides.

Every leather configuration in the Army is a dye and a doctrine on this one base: natural brown for the [Ranger Leather Kit](/armor/ranger-leather-kit), dark navy for the [Cobalt Ranger Kit](/armor/cobalt-ranger-kit), black for the [Ghost Kit](/armor/ghost-kit), silver under steel-blue cloth for the [Azure Covenant](/armor/azure-covenant-armor), and cream-clothed leather for the Field Menders of the Medical Corps.

## Kit Issue and Sidearms

All three kits are built around the standard [imperial sword](/armor/sidearms-and-weapons), which every soldier carries. The two lighter kits are issued more broadly with daggers, bows, and quivers, reflecting the scouting, skirmishing, and stealth roles for which they are designed. A soldier's kit announces their footing before a word is spoken — full plate for the line and the court, light steel for the road and the patrol, leather for the missions that are never meant to be seen.

## Runic Gear Tiers

Runic complexity scales with divisional rank rather than seniority. All magical soldiers receive rune-imbued gear — the Army does not distinguish between officers and enlisted when it comes to life-support equipment. What differs is the sophistication and power ceiling of the inscription.

| Tier | Division | Character |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Basic | [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) | Hard-coded script runes; single-function utility, designed for minimal magical training. |
| Standard | Specialist divisions | Variable-control capable runes with division-specific optimization — thermal absorption for Red, wind-resonant channels for Green. |
| Advanced | [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) | High-capacity variable-control runes; Maestro gauntlets designed for mid-combat spell-casting without interrupting weapon technique. |
| Specialized | [Azure Covenant](/grand-army/azure-covenant) | The most advanced casting tools outside of Purple — gauntlets engineered to focus raw mana into city-leveling spells while protecting the caster from their own output. |
| Precision | [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) | Biological precision runes, optimized for fine-grain thermal, kinetic, and atmospheric control at the scale of living tissue — the tightest tolerances in the catalog. |
| Masterwork | [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard) | Every piece individually rune-etched at Masterwork grade; the engineering standard by which all other runic work is measured. |
