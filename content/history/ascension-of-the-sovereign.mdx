*Certified account, ratified by the Grand Council in the first year of the current reign.*

Fourteen years ago the succession of Caeldrion broke sideways. The Archive's certified account is deliberately spare on the breaking — the deaths were sudden, the investigation is a sealed [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard) file, and the Empire's interest lies in what held rather than what failed. What held was procedure, and a twenty-seven-year-old.

## The Succession

The heir presumptive [ascended](/icons/the-sovereign) with a War Academy command rating five years old and no expectation of the throne. The Grand Council convened within the day under the Concord's succession clause; the [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard) sealed the [Citadel](/geography/caeldrion) and verified the line; confirmation came inside the month, unanimous on the second ballot. The annals record the new Sovereign's full first address to the Council. It was eleven words: *"The Empire is not interrupted. Publish the docket for the morning."*

## The Quiet Season

The first season of the reign is taught at the Academy as a stabilization campaign conducted without a battle:

- The Ledger audits were extended to the great houses — beginning, pointedly, with the Sovereign's own.
- The garrison commands were rotated on examination results published in full, the first such publication in the Empire's history.
- The court adopted the [Philosopher-King Doctrine](/icons/the-sovereign): alabaster over working cobalt, the throne held as a duty station.

## The Procession

The Ascension procession walked the [Grand Promenade](/geography/caeldrion) in silence — no music, by the new Sovereign's order, "until there is something finished to celebrate." The Archive notes that fourteen years on, the order has not been rescinded, and the Empire has come to regard the silence itself as the celebration.
