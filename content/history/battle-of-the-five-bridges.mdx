*Certified account, compiled from divisional after-action rulings. The Academy's annotated edition runs to six hundred pages; this entry is the Archive's public summary.*

Five bridges crossed the river Ael within a morning's march of one another, and an enemy field army three times the imperial strength needed at least one of them. Nine hours later it had none, and the Empire had the text on which a generation of officers has since been examined.

## The Composition

The imperial commander — a [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) Maestro whose name the rolls list, per doctrine, below the plan — treated the five crossings as one instrument:

1. **The approach** — [Green Ranger](/grand-army/green-rangers) pathfinders had sketched every crossing to the yard a week prior; the army arrived arranged, not assembled.
2. **The line** — the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) held the three central bridges at tempo, refusing the enemy the massed engagement their numbers wanted.
3. **The breach** — when the enemy committed to the Middle Bridge, the [Crimson Vanguard's](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) third serial took the gatehouse behind them and held it four counts past doctrine.
4. **The stroke** — the Legion's charge at failing light, uphill and nine hours into the action, broke the enemy center against their own crossing.

## The Ledger

The after-action ruling prices the victory in the Empire's plain arithmetic: three bridges damaged, one gatehouse ruined, casualties the examiners still call parsimonious against the result. The [Craftsman Corps](/grand-army/craftsman) rebuilt all five spans within the year; the Vanguard's twelve names remain sealed at the serial's own petition; one legionary of the line walked out of the gatehouse and was later named [Paladin of the Middle Bridge](/icons/the-paladins).

## In Doctrine

The battle is the Academy's standard examination text on economy of force, and the [reigning Sovereign](/icons/the-sovereign) — then a second-year cadet — famously failed the first paper set on it, a fact preserved in this Archive at the Sovereign's own instruction, under the heading *the examination system functions*.

> Archivist's note: four bridges over the Ael today bear division names. The fifth is called simply the Middle Bridge. Ask at any garrison why it is not renamed, and the answer is the same: it already has its name.
