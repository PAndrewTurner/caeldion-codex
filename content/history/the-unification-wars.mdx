The Empire of Caeldrion was not proclaimed — it was concluded, at the end of a generation of campaigns the annals group as the Unification Wars. The river kingdoms, the northern reaches beyond [Highfjord](/geography/highfjord), and the coastal cities from [Miada](/geography/miada) to the [Isles](/geography/the-sunlit-isles) entered the wars as rivals and left them as provinces.

## What the Wars Taught

Every pillar of imperial doctrine is a scar from this period, written into law so it would not need to be relearned:

- **Meritocracy** — the river kingdoms' armies were led by birth, and were beaten by it. The [War Academy's](/geography/caeldrion) blind examinations date from the third campaign.
- **Uniformity** — allied contingents in ancestral colors could not be told apart at a bowshot. The [Sea of Silver](/grand-army) — one steel, colored cloth for role alone — was first a signals reform and only later a philosophy.
- **Economy** — the wars were nearly lost twice, both times to supply. The [Craftsman Corps](/grand-army/craftsman) holds divisional rank because the annals are honest about which arm ended the wars.

## The Northern Winter

The siege of Highfjord — the wars' hardest chapter in the northern annals — held through a full winter on the Undercroft's fish and the garrison's arithmetic. The storm flag that flew that winter still flies over the Customs House, by charter, forever.

## Settlement

The wars ended in the Concord of the Rings, signed in the then-unfinished [Citadel](/geography/caeldrion). Its text is short; its first clause is the Empire's cornerstone: *service before birthright, in all things*.

> Archivist's note: no house is recorded as having won the Unification Wars. The Concord's signatories are listed by office held, not by name — the first document in this Archive to do so, and the reason all the rest do.
