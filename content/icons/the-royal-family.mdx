House Caelaris is the ruling dynasty of the Empire of Caeldrion. Nine years ago, the unexpected death of Sovereign Tiberius Caelaris thrust his eldest son, [Cassian](/icons/the-sovereign), onto the throne at twenty-seven and permanently altered the destinies of his four remaining children. Today the main branch consists of five siblings, and their lives are dictated entirely by the strict, meritocratic laws of the Empire and the rigid mathematics of the line of succession.

## The Visual Doctrines of the Crown

In Caeldrion a royal's uniform is their identity. The Crown enforces strict visual doctrines to communicate status, operational role, and ideological promise to the populace and the [Grand Army](/grand-army) alike. All royal armor in court bears the Gold Imperial Eagle on the breastplate; the materials around it tell the rest of the story:

- **The Sea of Silver** — the burnished war-steel of the enlisted Grand Army. Serving royals wear it to prove they bleed in the trenches alongside the commoners.
- **Alabaster War-Steel** — Masterwork, stark white armor reserved for non-combatant royals, the Sovereign, and honored [Paladins](/icons/the-paladins). It signifies an asset of the state, removed from the mud of the divisions.
- **Imperial Blue** — the exclusive color of the royal bloodline, representing absolute authority. The Sovereign extends this color to the [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy), marking its Officers and Maestros as the physical manifestation of the Crown's will.
- **The White Doctrine** — pure white silk tabards, underskirts, and capes worn by non-combatant royals. An ideological promise to the citizenry: purity, cleanliness, an unclouded vision of the Empire's future.
- **The Infiltration Leather Doctrine** — deep-territory reconnaissance units, royal or otherwise, replace the clanking silver plate with alchemically treated, hardened stealth leather for acoustic silence in the deep green.

## Prince Valerius Caelaris — The Heir Apparent

Age twenty-eight. Titles: the Heir Apparent, the Shadow Confidant. When Cassian ascended, Valerius instantly became immediate heir — and the [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard) pulled him from active duty that same day. Driven by profound loyalty, he willingly resigned his commission and stripped away his divisional blue. He holds Valedictorian honors from both the Imperial War Academy and the Imperial College, making him one of the most educated citizens of the Empire. Cassian originally wanted to name him Chief Advisor outright; the Imperial Command Council protested the optics of nepotism and autocracy, and Cassian — constitutionally free to appoint whom he pleased — chose to heed them. Valerius instead operates as the Sovereign's "shadow advisor," carrying the grueling intellectual work of running the Empire that no formal office requires him to.

He is bound by the White Doctrine: Masterwork Alabaster War-Steel over pure white silk, with a heavy white cape that signifies his immediate proximity to the throne — a mantle of responsibility that absorbs the Senate's political slights so his brothers can rule and fight.

## Prince Cyrus Caelaris — The Frontline Royal

Age twenty-eight, born mere minutes after Valerius. For the twenty-two years before their father's death, the twins were physically identical in every respect that mattered: same tutors, same Purple Guard combat instructors, same class at the Imperial War Academy, the same active commission as Cobalt Officers. Those few minutes of birth order then opened a permanent chasm between them. Where Valerius was pulled from the field, Cyrus went on to the captaincy and the frontline life they had both trained for.

Spared the immediate burden of the crown, Cyrus is a "working royal" — he inspects barracks, funds hospitals, and turns a devastatingly warm, room-charming smile toward statecraft. Where Valerius is the shield, Cyrus is the sword, and he does not confine himself to his own colors. He strips away his royal blue to fight in the bloody anonymity of the [Crimson Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard), where he met — and fell in love with — a massive Vanguard breacher who wields a kinetic warhammer. He also embeds with the [Green Rangers](/grand-army/green-rangers) for deep-territory reconnaissance, abandoning silver plate entirely for hardened stealth leather under the Infiltration Leather Doctrine, gear his sister has been known to help him obtain.

His standard heraldry is the burnished Sea of Silver over the heavy Imperial Blue cloth of the Cobalt Ascendancy — set aside for Vanguard red or Ranger green whenever he is moonlighting.

## Princess Lyra Caelaris — The Deep-Territory Ranger

Age twenty-four. Titles: the Deep-Territory Ranger. The only daughter of the main branch, and far enough removed from the immediate succession to choose her own path outright — she chose the [Green Rangers](/grand-army/green-rangers). She is deeply respected by the enlisted ranks, operating miles from the sapphire-lit halls of the Capital, and the Archive notes it is highly suspected she aids Cyrus in slipping the Citadel undetected for his Vanguard or Ranger moonlighting, helping him obtain both armor and supplies.

Her heraldry is the burnished Sea of Silver layered over Ranger Green silk, with scaled leather joints for the mobility and stealth her division's fieldcraft demands.

## Prince Elian Caelaris — The Cadet Royal

Age sixteen. Titles: the Cadet Royal, the Unproven Youth. The youngest of the siblings, and by design the Empire's "blank slate" — he has not yet come of age, nor faced the crucible of the [Imperial War Academy](/geography/caeldrion). His court portraits show a boy with a genuinely unburdened smile and faint freckles, untouched by the trauma of war or the paranoia of the throne.

He wears Alabaster War-Steel and pure white silk, but crucially without the heavy royal cape his older brothers carry — an absence that visually denotes his junior status. He has not yet earned a commission or a definitive place in the state's hierarchy, and the doctrine will not pretend otherwise on his behalf.

The Empire's front-line prohibition follows the position, not the person. Only the single individual immediately next in line to the throne is forbidden the field under any circumstances — their loss is a risk the Empire will not accept. Every other member of the Royal Family, including those further down the succession, may deploy if they personally choose to, and Cyrus and Lyra both have served with distinction. Should the heir apparent ever change, through birth or death, whoever newly stands immediately next in line is withdrawn from active service, and whoever is displaced from that position becomes free to serve in their place.

## Archival Records

A legendary official state portrait, *A Family of Service*, hangs in the Imperial War Academy. It places Cassian and Valerius on the left in Alabaster, and Cyrus and Lyra on the right in the burnished Silver of active service. Prince Elian is intentionally excluded — under the Empire's strict meritocracy, a royal cannot be featured in a portrait titled "Service" until they have officially matriculated into the military apparatus.

## Standing

The family's place in the meritocracy is deliberately narrow: they guarantee the succession, embody the court, and — where their own choices allow — serve in the line like anyone who has earned the right. Every member of the Royal Family is a capable magic user by design, the bloodline having married selectively across generations to preserve large Wells and efficient access; every royal child receives their combat and defensive instruction directly from Purple Guardsmen, ensuring each can defend themselves and understands the magic the Grand Army is built around.

> Archivist's note: white silk soils in an afternoon of honest work. The Citadel's laundries are, by design, among the busiest offices in the complex.
