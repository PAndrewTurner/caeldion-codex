The Royal Family of Caeldrion appears at court in Alabaster War-Steel over pure white silk — armored, always, and deployed never. Both halves of that sentence are law. The armor states that the house serves the Empire; the prohibition states that the Empire is not asked to bleed for the house.

## Court Doctrine

Where [the Sovereign](/icons/the-sovereign) crosses the alabaster with working cobalt, the family wears white over white: no divisional cloth, no campaign honors, no claim to a soldier's identity they have not earned by examination. The visual grammar of the court is exact —

- **Alabaster over white silk** — the family, present and serving, outside the chain of command.
- **Alabaster over divisional color** — a [Paladin](/icons/the-paladins), named for deeds.
- **Alabaster over cobalt** — the Sovereign alone.

## The Front-Line Prohibition

Members of the royal house may hold Academy ratings — several do — but statute bars them from front-line deployment. The reasoning is the Empire's habitual arithmetic: a royal in the line is a hostage the enemy prints its own coin from, and a command post no officer can speak plainly in. Royal service is discharged in the [Grand Council](/geography/caeldrion), the Academy's examination boards, and the endowment of the [Craftsman Corps'](/grand-army/craftsman) hospitals.

## Standing

The family's place in the meritocracy is deliberately narrow: they guarantee the succession and embody the court, and the [Purple Guard's Ledger](/grand-army/purple-guard) audits their households on the same schedule as any quartermaster's. The Archive records one exception granted in the current reign to the front-line prohibition. The petition was the Sovereign's own, made at twenty-seven, before ascension — and it was denied.

> Archivist's note: white silk soils in an afternoon of honest work. The court's laundries are, by design, the busiest office in the Citadel.
