The [Sea of Silver](/grand-army) erases the individual — with one deliberate exception. When a soldier's record passes beyond what rank can reward, the Empire does not promote them. It *names* them. A Paladin is a station, not a rank: the bearer keeps their military grade, their division, and their cloth, and is issued the one thing no one else may wear — Masterwork Alabaster War-Steel, matte white, with a gold-tasseled sash of Imperial Silk over the divisional color.

## The Station

The doctrine is precise about what a Paladin is for:

- **Independence** — a Paladin answers to the [Sovereign](/icons/the-sovereign) and the general staff, not to the local chain of command. They are committed where a battle needs a hinge.
- **Retained rank** — a Paladin-sergeant remains a sergeant. The alabaster confers no authority to command beyond their grade; it confers the authority to *matter*.
- **Visibility** — the white is meant to be seen. Where every soldier is silver, one alabaster figure in the line is a message legible to both armies.

## Naming

There is no application, no quota, and no posthumous award — the Empire names living soldiers only, on the sworn petition of those who stood in the same action. The Grand Council confirms; the [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard) verifies the record. In the current reign, eleven Paladins have been named. Six came from the [Iron Legion's](/grand-army/iron-legion) line.
