*"The Legion holds the line. The Cavalry holds the road."*

The Imperial Cavalry did not begin as a separate institution. It grew out of the [Iron Legion's](/grand-army/iron-legion) Mounted Legionaries — Black Legionnaires trained to ride for pursuit, rapid reinforcement, courier protection, road patrol, and battlefield exploitation, wearing black and serving under Legion discipline with the Legion's own understanding that mobility is valuable only in service of the line. As the Empire's roads lengthened, trade routes multiplied, and frontier patrols expanded, mounted service outgrew its origins, and the Sovereign elevated the tradition into its own auxiliary branch. The Cavalry inherited the Legion's discipline and built its own doctrine on top of it.

## Origin — From Mounted Legionaries to a Branch of Its Own

Mounted Legionaries were never cavalry in the traditional sense — they did not conduct mounted charges into formed infantry as an independent arm. They exploited the speed horses provided for specific tactical functions: pursuit of routed enemy forces after a [Crimson Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) assault, rapid reinforcement of a threatened line, courier protection, patrol support, and exploitation of breaches before an enemy could reorganize. The horse was a mobility platform, not the whole weapon, and a Legionary dismounted whenever close formation work was required. All mounted equipment of that era — saddle, leather barding, horse furniture — carried scripted defensive runes for passive, rechargeable protection, and that equipment is the direct technical ancestor of Cavalry gear today.

## Doctrine and Role

Where the Iron Legion holds the street, the gate, and the line, the Imperial Cavalry holds the road, the flank, and the distance between settlements. In campaign, the Cavalry performs the traditional mounted functions: screening the Army's movements, scouting routes at speed, protecting flanks, pursuing routed enemies, relaying field messages, disrupting enemy reconnaissance, and exploiting openings created by the main battle line. Troopers are not simply mounted infantry — though they dismount when terrain or tactical necessity demands it — and their defining value is operational mobility: projecting imperial force and authority faster than any foot formation can move.

Domestically, the Cavalry patrols the Empire's vast road network — anti-bandit operations, escort of travelers and high-value caravans, rapid response between towns, emergency courier protection, and reinforcement of isolated posts. Their presence reassures merchants, farmers, pilgrims, and magistrates traveling between communities that the Empire's roads are more than infrastructure. As the doctrine states them: they are promises, and the Cavalry exists to keep them.

## Equipment

All mounted equipment — saddle, leather barding, horse furniture — carries scripted defensive runes providing passive, rechargeable magical protection, the technical descendant of the original Mounted Legionary gear. Troopers wear the Light Steel Kit for mobility, in Iron Black cloth with gold trim marking their descent from, and continued kinship with, the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) — the only mounted unit designation in the Army carrying its own formal name and trim. Front-line heavy cavalry ride in full [War Plate](/arcanum/runic-armor) rather than the Light Kit used elsewhere in the branch. Cavalry mounts themselves are never armored to plate standard; barding remains leather and rune-scripted so the horse is never burdened to the rider's protection tier. The horse is a mobility platform, not the whole weapon — the trooper remains a complete soldier on foot when the situation requires it.

## Command and Council Standing

The Imperial Cavalry is an auxiliary branch rather than a full service branch, and it holds no dedicated seat of its own on the [Imperial Command Council](/governance/the-imperial-command-council). Its interests are represented jointly through the Sergeant Major of the Legion — reflecting the branch's direct descent from Legion doctrine — and the General of the Army, who carries Cavalry mobility considerations into broader campaign planning. The Cavalry maintains its own dedicated headquarters within the [Citadel](/geography/caeldrion) complex, distinct from the Black Legion Headquarters it grew out of, a reflection of its status as a fully independent branch even without independent Council representation.

## Selection

Cavalry troopers are drawn primarily from the Iron Legion's own ranks — the Black Crucible identifies an aptitude for riding, road-sense, and the specific discipline of operating at speed and at distance from the main formation, in much the same way it flags Vanguard, Ranger, or Cobalt aptitude. A Legionary who develops that aptitude over a career may petition transfer through the standard trial process. Because the branch's roots and its present doctrine both run through Legion discipline, a Cavalry trooper who later transfers back to Legion service, or onward into the Cobalt officer track, carries an unusually direct and well-understood record with them.

> Archivist's note: citizens who describe Cavalry patrols as "the Legion on horseback" are not wrong so much as a generation out of date. The doctrine has grown its own shape since; the resemblance is kinship, not identity.
