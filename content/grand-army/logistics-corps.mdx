The Lifeline is not a boast — it is named plainly on the Corps' own seal because it is a statement of function. The supply line, the runic manufacturing base, and the communications network: cut any one of them and the [Grand Army](/grand-army) begins dying immediately, invisibly, from the inside. Every inscription on every soldier's armor, every arrow, every ration, every [Field Corman](#the-field-corman) recharge in a muddy field, came from this Corps. It operates through three sub-divisions with distinct mandates and distinct colors, unified only by the umbrella they all serve.

> *The worst engagement I ever survived wasn't the Drake or the siege or the night action at Coldmere Pass. It was getting pushed into a supply depot raid with two Logistics Sergeants covering the exit. I watched a man in full plate get tapped on the shoulder and fly thirty feet. Then I watched the Sergeant pick up a supply crate and swing it like it weighed nothing. Then I stopped watching and started running.*
>
> — Corporal Edris Vane, Iron Legion, 3rd Battalion — Personal Account

## I. The Logistics Division

Buff-and-tan cloth marks the Army's operational supply chain — the institution ensuring every soldier in the field has what they need, every day, regardless of terrain, weather, or enemy action. It is not a combat division. It is the reason combat is possible, and in siege scenarios its capability is frequently the determining factor between success and failure.

### The Lighten — Mass Negation in Transport

The Division's primary magical application, Mass Negation, reduces the gravitational weight of objects to near-zero, letting a handful of Logistics soldiers move what a full company of non-magical porters could not. It runs at Tier I–II output for routine transport — sustainable indefinitely, the most economical magic use in the Army, consistent with Logistics doctrine in every other respect.

### The Shift — Weaponized Mass Negation

When Logistics soldiers find themselves in combat, which doctrine expects to be rare, the same physics becomes a profoundly disorienting weapon. **Negation** renders an armored enemy weightless, stripping their traction and force generation — a single punch does not injure, it launches, and the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) standing nearby handles what follows. **Amplification** increases a target's effective mass in a localized application — a dagger swung with amplified mass carries a warhammer's impact, and an opponent's own armor, amplified in place, buckles their knees under its own weight. A sufficiently skilled soldier can apply both simultaneously to different targets, lightening an ally's blade while weighting an enemy's armor — a force multiplier no enemy trained against conventional forces has a framework for.

### NCO Ranks and the Field Corman

The Logistics Division follows the Army's standard Private → Corporal → Sergeant progression, transferable across divisions with Transfer Training. Within it sits the **Field Corman** — a combat-trained specialist who deploys directly with field units to perform emergency repair of [runic gear](/arcanum/runic-armor) in active operational environments. A Field Corman handles standard repairs across every division, up to and including Masterwork-adjacent work for non-Purple-Guard equipment; Purple Guard Masterwork gear itself is beyond their scope and belongs to [the Craftsman](#iii-the-craftsman-the-apprentice) alone. Trained by Science Division Master Inscribers, the Field Corman is the Science Division's representative in the field — the one who understands, viscerally, what combat conditions do to a runic matrix that workshop testing never replicates.

## II. The Science Division

Turquoise cloth marks the Army's runic research and manufacturing institution — the source of every inscribed plate, every runic weapon, every [Covenant](/grand-army/azure-covenant) gauntlet, every Medical Corps closure bandage in the arsenal. Science Division personnel are never deployed to the field; their work requires controlled environments and institutional continuity that combat destroys, and they are protected by every other division simultaneously because losing that capacity would degrade all of them within months.

### Rank Structure

| Rank | Standing |
|---|---|
| Apprentice Smith | Entry level. Every Science Division member begins here regardless of prior military rank or civilian standing — a retired Cobalt General joining out of intellectual passion is an Apprentice Smith on day one. |
| Journeyman Inscriber | Competent independent practitioner, executing standard inscriptions without supervision. The bulk of the Division's working population. |
| Master Inscriber | Advanced practitioner capable of complex, division-specific inscription work; eligible for drafting by the Craftsman on Purple Guard project work. |
| Chief Artificer | Lord or Lady. Senior leadership of the Division, reporting directly to the Sovereign despite organizational proximity to Logistics. |

There is no aptitude-test shortcut into Science Division rank — inscription mastery is verifiable only through the work itself, so every entrant, whatever their background, starts at the bottom and proves the standard.

### What They Make

Production runs a full complexity spectrum: standard hard-coded scripts for Iron Legion spears and shields at high volume; variable-control specialist matrices for Ranger bows, Vanguard weapons, Engineering axes, Medical Corps leather, and Cobalt gauntlets; and Masterwork production for the [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard), executed exclusively by the Craftsman with Master Inscribers drafted for specific sub-tasks under direct supervision.

## III. The Craftsman & The Apprentice

Above the Science Division's rank structure entirely sit two singular positions — the Army's most critical single-point dependencies.

**The Craftsman** is one person, always — the definite article is precision, not convention. The depth of knowledge required to produce and maintain Masterwork Purple Guard equipment cannot be distributed across a committee. The Craftsman holds Lord or Lady title by personal elevation, is treated as a full Purple Guard peer in protocol and respect despite never sitting the [Violet Trials](/grand-army/purple-guard#selection-the-violet-trials), wears imperial purple, and may ground any piece of Purple Guard equipment for repair regardless of the using Guard's rank or preference.

**The Apprentice** is the Craftsman's identified heir, occupying a singular transitional status: above Science Division rank, below Purple Guard status, marked by a violet uniform — deep imperial purple mixed with cream white — belonging to neither group. The designation can be identified years or decades before succession is needed, approved by the Commander of the Guard and the Sovereign, and can be revoked if the Apprentice proves over time not to be the right choice. When the Craftsman's tenure ends, the Apprentice ascends and receives the Lord or Lady elevation.

The Army's most acute single-point vulnerability is not a Purple Guard death or a General's capture — it is a gap in Craftsman succession. An institution that fails to maintain an active Apprentice is failing one of its core obligations, and an Apprentice named fifteen years before they are needed is not premature. It is correct practice.

## IV. The Communications Division

Pale-yellow cloth marks the Empire's nervous system — the institution operating the obelisk network, a fixed magical infrastructure distributed across every town, fortress, and city, built jointly with the [Engineering Corps](/grand-army/engineering-corps) and the Logistics Division. Without it, Army command and control collapses to the speed of the fastest horse; with it, the [Sovereign](/icons/the-sovereign) knows what is happening anywhere in the Empire before an enemy can exploit the information gap.

The obelisks are inscribed with the same Voice application [Cobalt Officers](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) use to project command across a battlefield, encoded into permanent inscription and relayed node to node — a message from the northern frontier reaches the Capitol before a mounted rider has cleared the fortress gate. Because the obelisk itself sustains the output, Communications operators need precision and reliability rather than large Wells; senior operators with Variable Control can shape transmissions, apply encoding to sensitive messages, and repair damaged matrices.

The Division defers to the Quartermaster General for representation at the [Imperial Command Council](/governance/the-imperial-command-council) — the Communications Commander runs the network operationally but holds no Council seat. Deployment is the most distributed in the Army: two or three operators at a town obelisk, a single operator at a remote waypoint. Urban posts rely on the Iron Legion's existing city watch; isolated frontier and high-value hub obelisks receive dedicated Legion protective details, on the same logic that placed a Purple Guard sentinel at Winter's Gap. [The Eye](/governance/the-eye) uses a dedicated encrypted protocol within the network for its own traffic, transmitted by operators who never decode the content they carry.

> *People ask what we do. We make sure the General knows before the enemy does. That's it. That's the whole job.*
>
> — Communications Operator, 3rd Northern Network Station

## Heraldry

The Logistics Corps seal is teal-turquoise within a gold border of Celtic knotwork — a single image built from three layered elements: an anvil at the base, a runic obelisk rising from it, and a rune-inscribed supply crate suspended in a wavering distortion field at the obelisk's midpoint, Mass Negation rendered visible. The anvil is the Science Division's manufacturing foundation; the obelisk is the Communications network; the floating crate is field supply. Beneath them all: **The Lifeline** — the most literal motto in the Army's heraldry, because it is simply what the Corps is.

> Archivist's note: petitioners occasionally ask why the Craftsman wears Purple Guard cloth while serving under a Logistics-adjacent Council portfolio. The answer is elevation, not transfer — the Craftsman was never Purple Guard by the Violet Trials, and is a peer of the Guard by a different and equally absolute standard.
