The Cobalt Ascendancy is the Grand Army's answer to a simple observation: the deadliest thing on any field is a mind that can read it. The Ascendancy trains that mind, arms it with [Hybrid Weaving](/arcanum/the-weave), and issues it a blue cloth. Every graduate of the [Imperial War Academy](/geography/caeldrion) who tests into the command track passes through Cobalt instruction; [the Sovereign](/icons/the-sovereign) wears the deep cobalt sash to this day.

## Doctrine

Ascendancy doctrine is written around the phrase *battle as authorship*. A Cobalt officer does not react to an engagement — they compose it, holding the [Iron Legion's](/grand-army/iron-legion) line, the [Crimson Vanguard's](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) breach, and their own weaving as movements of a single work.

- **Hybrid Weaving** — the simultaneous conduct of sword-work and casting, possible only because [runic armor](/arcanum/runic-armor) sinks [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn) faster than a duelist generates it.
- **The Maestro cadre** — senior weavers rated to conduct battalion-scale battle-magic without relay casters.
- **The Azure Covenant** — the Ascendancy's oath-bound inner circle, from which field marshals are drawn by examination, never by appointment.

## Selection

Cobalt candidacy requires a double qualification unique in the Grand Army: a command rating from the War Academy and a stable [mana well](/arcanum/mana-wells) measured above the seventh threshold. Fewer than one in two hundred aspirants hold both. The examination is blind; the graders never learn a candidate's name, only their number.

## Notable Engagements

The Ascendancy's composition of the [Battle of the Five Bridges](/history/battle-of-the-five-bridges) remains the Academy's standard text on economy of force: five crossings held for nine hours against a treble enemy, at a cost the examiners still call parsimonious.

> Archivist's note: the epithet "Lords of War" is popular, not official. The division's rolls list no lords — only ratings earned and re-earned.
