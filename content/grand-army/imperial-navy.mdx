*"Any power that cannot defend its waters cannot defend its prosperity."*

The Imperial Navy is a full branch of service, distinct from the land forces of the [Grand Army](/grand-army) but bound to the same imperial purpose: defense of the realm, protection of trade, and preservation of the Sovereign's peace wherever imperial authority reaches across water. The Empire is not merely a land power. [Miada](/geography/miada) is its premier port; Emberhall is a rebuilt coastal and maritime center absorbed after the [Terran War](/history/the-unification-wars); [the Sunlit Isles](/geography/the-sunlit-isles) guard the southeastern approaches with fortified harbors; and Riverwatch, though inland, supplies the timber and rivercraft the fleet depends on. The Empire's wealth moves by road, river, and sea, and the Navy exists because a power that cannot defend its waters cannot defend its prosperity.

## Mandate

The Navy guards the nation's coasts, protects shipping lanes, and conducts anti-piracy and anti-incursion patrols. Its defining campaign is the [Terran War](/history/the-unification-wars): it was the Imperial Navy that protected the then-independent Sunlit Isles from Terran aggression and, in the same operation, denied Terran forces a coastal invasion of Miada — forcing the enemy onto a land campaign on ground of the Empire's choosing. That naval denial is regarded as the strategic hinge of the entire war, the precondition that made the land engagement at [Highfjord](/geography/highfjord) and the [Battle of the Five Bridges](/history/battle-of-the-five-bridges) possible on terms the Empire could win.

## Shipbuilding

Naval construction concentrates in three centers, each contributing a different strength to the fleet. Miada builds the most refined vessels — merchant escorts, command ships, diplomatic craft — drawing on generations of harbor culture and guild skill. Emberhall builds with urgency and ambition: ship repair, coastal-defense vessels, and patrol ships, each launch a proof that the city once allied against the Empire is now a contributor to its strength. Riverwatch provides the inland backbone, moving Riverlands timber toward the coast and producing river vessels, barges, patrol craft, and hull components. No imperial fleet is built only at the sea — it begins inland, where forests become beams, masts, ribs, and hull sections long before a keel touches water.

## Equipment

Naval personnel — sailors, marines, and shipboard archers alike — wear the Light Steel Kit rather than full War Plate, for the plainest reason in the fleet's doctrine: a sailor who goes over the side in full plate does not surface. The Light Kit's full-grade steel breastplate and reduced peripheral coverage preserve the protection that matters while keeping mobility on a moving deck and survivability in the water. The Navy does maintain War Plate, worn for court, parade, and diplomatic occasions like every other division — it is simply never fielded at sea. Naval magic follows the same principle as all imperial warfare: mana as physics under discipline, not spectacle, applied to shipboard combat exactly as the [Crimson Vanguard's](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) thermal, kinetic, and explosive profiles are applied ashore.

## Officer Corps and Council Standing

Naval officers — Captain, Commander, and Admiral — are [Cobalt Officers](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) and wear cobalt blue rather than the Marine-Blue-trimmed Legion black of the enlisted and NCO ranks, mirroring how the [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) supplies trained officers across the Army generally. Unlike the Imperial Cavalry, the Navy is a full service branch: the Admiral of the Navy holds a complete, independent seat on the [Imperial Command Council](/governance/the-imperial-command-council). The Admiral maintains a headquarters within the [Citadel](/geography/caeldrion) complex for Council and Sovereign access, but the Navy's main operational headquarters sits in Miada, the Empire's premier port, kept in constant contact with the Capitol through the Communications Division's [obelisk network](/grand-army/logistics-corps#iv-the-communications-division).

## Selection and Career Web

Naval enlisted service draws recruits directly from the Black Crucible's broader intake, much as the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) does, with an assessment weighted toward the specific temperament shipboard service demands — steadiness in confined and moving spaces, tolerance for isolation, and comfort operating far from the obelisk network's near-instant reach. Naval officers pass through the same [Imperial War Academy](/geography/caeldrion) curriculum as every other Cobalt-commissioned officer, wearing Navy trim from the Crucible through graduation before it is replaced by full cobalt cloth. A soldier's path into naval service, and back out of it into land command, follows the Army's standard inter-division transfer doctrine — a career at sea is a career inside the Grand Army, not apart from it.

> Archivist's note: the Sunlit Isles' willing accession to the Empire is remembered locally as the day the fleet arrived rather than the day the treaty was signed. The Navy's own histories record both dates; the Isles keep only one.
