Where the [Grand Army](/grand-army) faces outward, the Sovereign's Arcanum faces every direction at once. The Guard exists for three duties and no others: the person of [the Sovereign](/icons/the-sovereign), the integrity of the officer corps, and the death of treason. Its writ supersedes divisional command. A sergeant of the Purple may detain a colonel of the Cobalt, and has.

## Mandate

The Arcanum's charter, ratified in the first year of the current reign, names it the "final instrument of imperial trust." In practice this resolves to three standing operations:

- **The Aegis Watch** — close protection of the Sovereign and the [Royal Family](/icons/the-royal-family), integrated with the [Gold Aegis ward runes](/arcanum/runic-armor) inlaid in the Sovereign's war-steel.
- **The Ledger** — continuous audit of promotion, procurement, and supply against corruption. Merit is the Empire's spine; the Ledger keeps it unbent.
- **The Hunt** — investigation and termination of treason. Files under this heading are sealed above this clearance.

## Selection

No one applies to the Purple Guard. Candidates are identified by the Ledger itself: soldiers whose records show competence without patronage, and loyalty without ambition. Induction erases prior divisional affiliation — the deepest expression of the [Sea of Silver doctrine](/grand-army), in which even elite status is only another issued cloth.

## Issued War-Steel

Guard kit is standard burnished war-steel with imperial purple cloth. The stealth pattern, called the Ghost Kit within the division, replaces plate with alchemically darkened weave for work the Empire does not announce.

## Notable Actions

The Guard's confirmed record is deliberately thin. Two entries are public by decree: its role in securing the succession during the [Ascension of the Sovereign](/history/ascension-of-the-sovereign), and the dissolution of the Gilded Chain procurement ring, which returned the cost of two legions to the treasury.
