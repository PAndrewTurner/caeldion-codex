The Grand Army marches on roads the Green Rangers walked a season earlier. The green cloth marks the division that operates past the last supply post and the last reliable map: deep reconnaissance, route-proving, and long-range interdiction across the frontier territories from the [Highfjord](/geography/highfjord) passes to the approaches of [the Sunlit Isles](/geography/the-sunlit-isles).

## Doctrine

Ranger doctrine inverts the army's mass: where the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) is built to be seen and reckoned with, the Rangers are built to be doubted. A Ranger element reports, shapes, and withdraws; a fight it cannot decline is a fight it has already failed to plan.

- **The Deep Watch** — standing patrols beyond imperial borders, rotating on a season clock.
- **Pathfinder detachments** — attached to field commands to prove routes, fords, and passes ahead of movement.
- **Treated leathers** — Ranger kit substitutes alchemically hardened leather for plate where silence outweighs stopping power; the [arming doublet](/arcanum/arming-doublets) beneath is standard issue.

## Selection

Ranger assessment is the army's longest: a full season in the deep country under instructor shadow. It tests navigation, forage, silence, and — the trait the cadre weighs heaviest — the judgment to come home with the report rather than die improving it. Advancement follows patrol leadership, and the division's rolls note it plainly: rank in the Rangers is a count of soldiers brought back.

## Notable Actions

Ranger honors are, by the division's own preference, mostly negative space: the ambush not sprung, the pass not held against the army because a Deep Watch element had unheld it the week before. The public record credits Ranger pathfinding for the approach march that opened the [Battle of the Five Bridges](/history/battle-of-the-five-bridges) with every crossing already sketched to the yard.

> Archivist's note: Ranger patrol logs are filed under seal for one generation. The division argues, and the Archive concurs, that a map of where the Empire looks is a map of what it fears.
