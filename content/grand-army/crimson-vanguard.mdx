The Crimson Vanguard exists for the worst minute of any battle: the one spent inside the enemy's strongest point. Red cloth over the heaviest war-steel the [Craftsman Corps](/grand-army/craftsman) can hang on a human frame, the Vanguard is committed only where the [Iron Legion's](/grand-army/iron-legion) pressure has fixed the enemy and a [Cobalt](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) commander has chosen the seam.

## Doctrine

Vanguard doctrine is written in a single word: *through*. A breach unit does not fight for ground — it fights for a hole, holds it for the count, and hands it to the Legion behind. Everything else in the division's manual is the arithmetic of that count.

- **Breach serials** — twelve-soldier elements sized to a standard gate span, drilled to rotate the point every thirty heartbeats.
- **Rune-scored plate** — Vanguard war-steel carries the deepest [heat-sink channels](/arcanum/runic-armor) in the army, rated against siege-fire and the caster's own [Burn](/arcanum/the-burn) alike.
- **The Second Man rule** — no Vanguard soldier enters a breach first twice in one action; the division spends courage like ammunition, by count.

## Selection

The Vanguard recruits exclusively from soldiers with line service — it wants no one who has not stood behind a wall before being asked to go through one. Its physical standard is the army's highest; its examination ends with a live breach under instructor fire. Merit in the Vanguard is measured in the only currency it respects: the count held.

## Notable Engagements

At the [Battle of the Five Bridges](/history/battle-of-the-five-bridges), the Vanguard's third serial took and held the Middle Bridge gatehouse for four counts past doctrine — the margin, the after-action ruling states, on which the entire composition balanced. The serial's twelve names are sealed; the Vanguard asked that the record credit "the serial, entire."

> Archivist's note: applicants citing the Vanguard's casualty rate as evidence of glory are returned to their divisions. The Vanguard's own manual calls the rate a defect it drills daily to reduce.
