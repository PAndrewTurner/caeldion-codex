Every plan the Grand Army has ever executed contains an unwritten first line: *the Legion holds*. The Iron Legion is the Empire's frontline infantry — the largest division by an order of magnitude, the black cloth against the silver, the wall behind which the [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) composes and ahead of which the [Crimson Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) breaks.

## Doctrine

Legion doctrine is attritional only in the eyes of amateurs. Its actual discipline is *tempo denial*: the line advances, halts, and holds at rhythms chosen to starve an enemy of the engagement they want. Legionary drill assumes the [Sea of Silver](/grand-army) in its purest form — soldiers interchangeable, formations modular, no maneuver dependent on any single name.

- **The Line** — rank-and-file heavy infantry in full war-steel.
- **The Shadow cadre** — night-action companies issued darkened plate for approach marches and silent seizure of ground.
- **Sub-units** — sapper, pike, and shield-wall specializations rotate through the line rather than standing apart from it.

## Selection

The Legion takes the Empire's broadest intake and applies its narrowest standard: the drill passes or it does not. Advancement is by demonstrated steadiness — the Legion promotes the soldier the line follows when the noise starts. More Legion sergeants have been raised to [Paladin](/icons/the-paladins) than from any other division.

## Notable Engagements

The Legion's charge at the [Battle of the Five Bridges](/history/battle-of-the-five-bridges) — delivered uphill, in failing light, after nine hours in the line — is the engagement every recruit learns on the first day and understands only after their first action. The Legion lists its honors by bridge, wall, and gate held, not enemies slain.

> Archivist's note: the Legion's rolls do not record individual heroics. Where a name must be cited, the entry reads "a legionary of the line."
