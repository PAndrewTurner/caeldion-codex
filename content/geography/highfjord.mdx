Highfjord lies in the central Empire, within the Riverlands, between [Caeldrion](/geography/caeldrion) and [Miada](/geography/miada). Long before the city existed, water cut the landscape into dramatic channels — sheer cliffs, narrow passages, deep blue corridors carved by the Empire's ancient rivers through mountain and stone. Generations of engineers, masons, merchants, and civic planners transformed that natural wonder into one of the Empire's most beautiful inland cities, built along and around the fjords rather than in spite of them. Its civic maxim is carved into stone at the Customs House: *We do not conquer the mountains. We collaborate with them.*

## The Bridges

Highfjord is famous above all for its bridges — great spans crossing the fjords high above the water, tall enough for ships to pass beneath them. They are not merely stone. Each is a work of [Engineering Corps](/grand-army/engineering-corps) art and runic reinforcement, magically supported and structurally bound, maintained through a combination of civic engineering, inspection, and arcane load management. Their arches carry roads, footpaths, processions, caravans, and sometimes entire market rows across heights no unaided masonry could hold.

### The Five Spans and the Battle That Named Them

The five widest bridges are the city's defining monument. During the [Terran War](/history/the-unification-wars), the Terran army's advance was funneled by the vertical geography of Highfjord into these five crossings — the only way across the fjords for an army of any size. Rather than destroy three generations of the Engineering Corps' own work to deny the enemy the crossing, five Cobalt Maestros stepped from the retreating imperial lines and walked alone onto the five spans, holding them for hours against a Terran force three times the Empire's strength. The full account, including Captain Aleksey's stand on the widest bridge and the six hundred kills credited to it, is certified under [the Battle of the Five Bridges](/history/battle-of-the-five-bridges). Today the five spans bear the names of the warriors who held them; the grandest is the Aleksey Bridge.

### The Council of Maestros

Each year, on the cliffside where the imperial forces held, serving and veteran Maestros from across the Empire gather at Highfjord for the Council of Maestros. It holds no command authority and exercises no function within the broader [Grand Army](/grand-army) — it is a gathering of fellowship and memory, where Maestros who earned their cobalt blue through entirely different paths — former Vanguard, Rangers, Legionnaires, and Officers alike — share new techniques, honor the fallen of the Five Bridges, and mark the year's new elevations. For that one weekend, as hundreds of the Empire's most lethal individual combatants walk its streets, Highfjord becomes the second most dangerous place in the known world — eclipsed only by the Imperial Throne Room and its [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard).

### The Vertical Supply Chain

Moving cargo from the river docks up the sheer cliffs to the upper terraces is Logistics Division work: practitioners apply the Lighten to reduce the gravitational weight of goods, keeping the terraces supplied without endless manual haulage — the same Engineering Corps ingenuity that raised the bridges applied, at a smaller scale, to the daily business of a working city.

## Standing

Highfjord's economy rests on tourism, commerce, river passage, bridge tolls, hospitality, artisan work, engineering maintenance, and transport. Ships pass beneath its high bridges while road traffic crosses above — a city of layers: water below, stone above, roads in the air, markets on the terraces, and bells echoing across the fjords. If [Miada](/geography/miada) is sea and citrus, and [the Capital](/geography/caeldrion) is mountain and stone, Highfjord is water suspended between cliffs.

> Archivist's note: the Engineering Corps' load ratings for the five spans are a matter of public record; their safety margins are not. The Corps builds the way the Legion holds — with more in reserve than it announces.
