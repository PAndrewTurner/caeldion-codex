Highfjord is what happens when the [Craftsman Corps](/grand-army/craftsman) is given a cliff and a century. The northern fortress-port is cut into the fjord walls in stacked terraces — quays and undercrofts at the waterline, markets and garrison at the shoulder, batteries at the crown — commanding the one deep-water passage into the Empire's northern reach.

## The Gate of the North

All northern trade passes under Highfjord's guns and through its Customs House, and the city's charter is frank about the arrangement: the Empire keeps the passage safe, and the passage keeps the Empire's ledgers full. The garrison is anchored by the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion), with a [Green Ranger](/grand-army/green-rangers) wing working the passes above the tree line.

## Works of Note

- **The High Bridge** — the narrows' single span, an Engineering Directorate masterwork rated to bear a division in march order.
- **The Cliff Batteries** — gun terraces cut at three heights; their interlocking fields close the fjord to anything the Empire has not invited.
- **The Covered Market** — the north's trading floor, roofed against eight months of weather.
- **The Echo Chamber** — a natural gallery in the cliff where the harbor watch reads incoming ships by sound long before sight; the Rangers train listeners there.
- **The Fisherman's Undercroft** — the waterline town beneath the town, older than the fortress above it and, by charter, exempt from its rents.

## Garrison Life

Highfjord's terraces grow more under glass than the north thought possible, and its Wayfarer's Inn is the most stamped address in any Ranger's leave record. The city withstood its only modern investment during the [siege remembered in the northern annals](/history/the-unification-wars); the Customs House still flies the storm flag that flew that winter.

> Archivist's note: the Engineering Directorate lists the High Bridge's load rating in the public record and its safety margin nowhere. The Directorate builds the way the Legion holds — with more in reserve than it announces.
