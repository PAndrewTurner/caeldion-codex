Miada is the Empire with its collar loosened. The southern port climbs its hillsides in white terraces above a harbor that never quite sleeps, and the city's standing joke — that the garrison keeps the peace and Miada keeps the garrison entertained — appears, lightly edited, in three official reports held by this Archive.

## The Port

Miada's harbor is the Empire's warm-water counterweight to [Highfjord](/geography/highfjord): the southern trade — grain, wine, dye, and the coastal traffic of [the Sunlit Isles](/geography/the-sunlit-isles) — clears through its quays. The port's charter grants it the Empire's lightest customs seal and, not coincidentally, its busiest.

## The City of Fountains

Miada's aqueducts feed a fountain in every square — the Engineering Directorate maintains them under the same contract as the harbor moles, and the city repays the courtesy by treating the engineers' guild-marks as minor shrines. Fountain Square, the largest, doubles as the city's forum, market overflow, and, four nights in five, its dance floor.

- **The Terraces** — residential Miada, stacked to the ridgeline, whitewashed by ordinance and repainted by argument.
- **Fountain Square** — forum and festival ground; the garrison parades here exactly twice a year and is applauded like a visiting troupe.
- **The Night Culture** — Miada's lamps burn until morning; its musicians' guild is chartered, examined, and ranked, because Miada is still the Empire, and even joy is meritocratic here.

## Standing

Miada fields no division of its own but feeds several: the port levy staffs the fleet's southern squadron, and the [Green Rangers](/grand-army/green-rangers) recruit their coast-watchers from Miadan fishing families who read the water the way archivists read a ledger.
