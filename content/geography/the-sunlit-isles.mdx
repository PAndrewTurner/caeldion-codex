The Sunlit Isles are the Empire's last certain landfall — an archipelago of coral passages and deep-water harbors strung across the southern approaches. Past them lies water the [Green Rangers'](/grand-army/green-rangers) coast-watchers describe with the cartographer's oldest honesty: incompletely.

## The Chain

- **Coralwind Harbor** — the fleet's forward anchorage, sheltered inside a reef the pilots' guild licenses passage through one hull at a time.
- **Sunhaven Port** — the civilian capital of the chain; the [Miada](/geography/miada) trade's far terminus and the sunniest customs post in the imperial service.
- **The Azure Passage** — the deep channel threading the chain; the only route a ship of the line can swim, and therefore the only route the Empire truly guards.
- **Fort Dawnshield** — the Engineering Directorate's southernmost work, holding the Passage's mouth.

## Fort Dawnshield

Dawnshield is [Craftsman Corps](/grand-army/craftsman) doctrine in coral stone: a low, thick, unglamorous work whose batteries command the Passage's mouth and whose cisterns could outlast a two-year investment. Its garrison rotates through from the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion), and its standing order is one sentence long: the Passage stays imperial.

## The Frontier

The Isles are where imperial certainty thins by design. Survey beyond the chain is a Ranger task, seasonal and sealed; the fleet's charts of the far water carry the Archive's rarest stamp — *provisional* — and the Empire, which hates the word, keeps funding the voyages that might one day remove it.

> Archivist's note: petitioners asking what lies beyond the Isles receive the Archive's standard answer: "The next expedition's roster is posted each spring. Literacy in navigation is a prerequisite. So is returning."
