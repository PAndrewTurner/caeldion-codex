*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. VI: The Arming Layer. Distribution: general.*

The least glamorous garment in the Empire is the one the [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) credits with the most saved lives. The arming doublet — the soldiers' word is still gambeson — is the quilted under-layer worn beneath every suit of war-steel: aramid fiber, alchemically treated in the [Logistics Corps'](/grand-army/logistics-corps) baths, cut close and laced to carry the plate's weight on the body's strong lines.

## Function

The doublet does three jobs at once, and doctrine refuses to rank them:

- **Kinetic absorption** — the treated weave spreads and soaks impact that the plate transmits; the blow that dents war-steel bruises instead of breaking.
- **Thermal insulation** — [runic armor](/arcanum/runic-armor) works by moving [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn) into the plate. The doublet is the one-way valve that keeps a heat-loaded plate from simply handing the injury back.
- **Foundation** — channel contact-points seat against the doublet's fitted panels at spine, forearm, and collar. A doublet laced wrong breaks the circuit; inspection doctrine therefore begins at the laces, not the plate.

## Treatment

Raw aramid cloth enters the treatment baths supple and leaves with its virtues fixed: proof against ember and splash, dimensionally stable under the plate's heat cycling, and — the alchemists' quiet triumph — washable. The formula is a fabrication annex; the fact that there is a formula is not, because the Directorate found that soldiers care for a garment better when told it is difficult to replace.

## The Doublet and the Backup Layer

Soldiers who also carry [runic body inscription](/arcanum/runic-body-work) as insurance against lost plate should note that the two systems are not interchangeable substitutes for one another — a doublet with no plate over it insulates nothing, and a tattoo circuit with no doublet beneath a recovered suit still needs the arming layer's kinetic protection once the soldier re-arms. Doctrine treats them as a stack, not a choice: tattoos cover the gap between losing armor and recovering it; the doublet is what makes the recovered armor survivable to wear again.

## Issue and Care

Every soldier of the [Grand Army](/grand-army) draws two doublets and is drilled to rotate them; a wet arming layer insulates like a confession — badly. Field repair is authorized at the seam only. Channel-panel damage returns the garment to a Corps workshop, and the manual's phrasing on this point has never required revision: "The doublet is half your armor. It is the half you can ruin with a needle."

> Directorate note: veterans who sleep in their doublets in the field are following doctrine. Recruits who sleep in them in barracks are following veterans, which is also doctrine, eventually.
