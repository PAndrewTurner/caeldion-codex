*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. XI: Mana Manipulation and Sensing Doctrine. Distribution: general. City-scale sensing authorization annexes restricted to Council record-holders.*

Mana manipulation is a distinct magical discipline that operates on a different principle from every other application in this system. Standard magic converts mana into a specific form of energy — thermal, gravitational, electrical, void. That conversion is what produces heat as a byproduct, what triggers [the Burn's](/arcanum/the-burn) staged presentation, what [runic armor's](/arcanum/runic-armor) defensive inscription was engineered to manage. Mana manipulation skips the conversion entirely. The practitioner exerts raw mana directly as force — the mana itself is the weapon, the pressure, the detection field. No conversion. No application type. The mana is the force.

## A Different Cost Profile

Mana manipulation does generate heat and fatigue — any expenditure of energy has a thermal byproduct — but at a substantially lower rate than conversion-based applications. Where a Tier III sustained application produces rapid heat accumulation that the armor's inscription must aggressively manage, mana manipulation produces slow, steady heat accumulation closer to sustained physical exertion. The practitioner fatigues. They warm. They deplete their [well](/arcanum/mana-wells). But the spike-and-accumulation pattern that drives Burn stage progression is largely absent. A practitioner sustaining Echolocation Mapping for four hours is not approaching a Burn threshold — they are approaching exhaustion.

## Why the Discipline Is Rare

Mana manipulation requires either a natural tendency toward mana extrusion — an instinctive awareness of mana as an external force that some practitioners develop early and most never develop at all — or extensive specific training by someone who already possesses it. Large well depth alone is insufficient. The combination of sufficient well depth *and* the specific aptitude or training for external mana extrusion produces practitioners capable of the discipline's techniques. Without both, the techniques are inaccessible regardless of raw magical power.

## Mana Buffer

The foundational counter-technique and the entry point into mana manipulation practice. The practitioner projects raw mana outward from their body in a thin, sustained layer — not a directed application, but an ambient field creating a pressure boundary between the practitioner and incoming mana manipulation force. The buffer does not stop a physical attack, and it does not absorb conversion-type magical applications. It specifically resists incoming mana manipulation pressure — Submission force, Explosive Release — by meeting that pressure with opposing pressure at the boundary layer.

The critical variable is timing. A practitioner who establishes their buffer before Submission pressure has accumulated projects against minimal resistance — the buffer holds easily, for modest mana expenditure. A practitioner who waits until Submission pressure has already established — until they are already at reduced mobility — projects their buffer against accumulated force, at substantially higher cost. The window of recognition matters enormously, which is why detection awareness is trained alongside the buffer technique itself, not after it.

## Mana Submission

The practitioner exerts raw mana outward as an ambient pressure field — directional, a cone or wave toward a specific target, or omnidirectional, a sphere affecting everything within range simultaneously. The field exerts a weight-like force on everything within it: not targeted at specific anatomy, not interacting with a target's magical systems, not producing heat or light. A pressure the target's body experiences as omnidirectional weight. Legs buckle first, carrying the load; the spine follows. A physically strong target in excellent condition has no advantage over a weak one — the pressure does not care about muscle.

Effective range and intensity scale entirely with the practitioner's well depth and sustained output. A standard [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) Maestro-scale well produces pressure sufficient to force untrained soldiers to their knees and slow trained practitioners. A [Sovereign's Arcanum](/grand-army/purple-guard) Guard-scale well produces pressure that a practitioner with a modest well cannot buffer against regardless of technique — the disparity in available mana is too large for the buffer to overcome.

The technique produces no visible magical signature — no light, no heat shimmer, no discharge color. The target simply stops being able to stand. A Purple Guard using Mana Submission against a court assailant appears to be doing nothing; the assailant appears to be experiencing a medical episode. The technique's invisibility is itself a tactical asset in protective and diplomatic contexts.

Counter-options are limited to establishing a Mana Buffer before pressure accumulates, an Explosive Release of sufficient magnitude to overwhelm the submission gradient, or possessing sufficient well depth that the practitioner's own ambient mana naturally resists the incoming pressure. None are available to a target without significant magical capability and specific training.

## Mana Explosive Release

The practitioner releases a large volume of raw mana outward simultaneously — not a sustained field, but a burst. The burst can be omnidirectional, a sphere expanding from the practitioner's body, or directed, a focused wave toward a specific target. Two tactical functions:

- **Counter to Submission** — an omnidirectional burst of sufficient magnitude overwhelms an incoming Submission gradient; the pressure sphere inverts momentarily, the Submission pressure is pushed back, and the practitioner has a window to move, reposition, or establish a Buffer before the submitting practitioner re-establishes. The burst depletes faster than sustained output, so if Submission re-establishes before the window is used, the practitioner has spent significant mana for seconds of freedom. Follow-through determines whether the counter succeeds.
- **Concussive Battlefield Attack** — directed outward as a focused wave, the Release produces concussive force that strikes targets physically: not magically targeted, not interacting with runic systems, but pure physical impact from mana pressure. Unshielded soldiers are thrown backward and formation integrity collapses. Against a shielded practitioner with an active Buffer, the Release must overwhelm the Buffer before it reaches them, requiring significantly more output.

The Explosive Release produces a visible shockwave effect — the mana pressure wave registers as a distortion in the air at the moment of release, similar to a concussive blast — but no colored discharge and no thermal signature. The shockwave and its effects are the only evidence it occurred.

## Mana Sensing — Basic

The practitioner extends raw mana outward as a passive ambient field, not projecting force but extending awareness. The field registers contact and movement as tactile information returning to the practitioner — not vision, not hearing, but a tactile sense analogous to proprioception extended beyond the body's physical boundaries. When something enters the field, moves through it, or exerts its own mana within it, the practitioner feels it the way one feels someone touching a hand.

Basic sensing provides presence, movement, direction, approximate size, and whether a target is mana-active — nothing more. A Citadel sensing-shift Guard knows fourteen people are in the east corridor, three are moving, and one has a mana signature consistent with a practitioner; they do not know what those people look like. The Citadel sensing shift system maintains overlapping sensing spheres tiling the entire complex continuously, rotated to keep every Guard at functional precision as the field's four-hour sustained cost approaches exhaustion rather than [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn). Ranger perimeter sensing in field operations applies the same technique at smaller scale, and the Inquisition's city-scale linked sensing pools multiple Guards' fields into a network covering an entire city, filtering for a specific signature in pursuit operations.

Basic sensing produces no visual signature and cannot be detected by most practitioners; a sophisticated practitioner may notice a subtle ambient quality difference — like the difference between fresh and pressurized air — detectable only by someone paying specific attention for it.

## Mana Sensing — Echolocation Mapping

The advanced technique, categorically different from basic sensing in both mechanism and output. Where basic sensing extends mana passively and registers what disturbs it, Echolocation Mapping pulses mana outward in calibrated frequencies and reads what returns. The returning mana carries information about what it contacted — shape, density, material, precise geometry — which the practitioner's mind assembles into a spatial map, separate from what the eyes see.

- **Penetrates obstacles** — mana passes through walls, floors, and ceilings; a practitioner mapping a building maps the building, not the room they occupy.
- **Requires no light** — darkness, smoke, fog, and magical visual concealment do not affect the returning data.
- **Distinguishes materials** — flesh, stone, metal, wood, and magical materials return mana differently; a skilled practitioner identifies what they are mapping materially as well as spatially.
- **Updates continuously** — not a snapshot; the map tracks movement in real time.
- **Resolution degrades with distance** — close range resolves a sword versus a dagger under a cloak; maximum range drops to presence and approximate size.

The technique requires Purple Guard-level well depth for meaningful operational range and precision — the calibrated pulses must be precisely tuned, requiring Variable Control precision most practitioners never develop — plus the same natural manipulation tendency or extensive training the discipline demands generally. A Purple Guard sustaining Echolocation Mapping of the entire Citadel complex during a security emergency has complete real-time awareness of every living thing in the complex: location, material, and movement.

## Mana Signature

Every practitioner produces a specific quality of mana output that distinguishes them from every other practitioner — a signature as individual as a voice. Sensing practitioners read these signatures in the field they maintain; the Citadel sensing-shift Guards catalogue every regular occupant's signature over months of service, so an unfamiliar signature entering the Citadel is an immediate alert regardless of what it belongs to.

The signature has forensic applications beyond sensing. The [Medical Corps'](/grand-army/medical-corps) Well Interference sub-specialty, which already distinguishes voluntary depletion from traumatic external drain, can identify specific practitioners from the signature left in a sealed or [drained](/arcanum/mana-draining) well — the characteristic quality of how that specific practitioner's mana interacted with the target's well architecture. A sealed Guard whose sealing practitioner left a recoverable signature has provided evidence that can identify the sealer, and the same forensic function extends to [storage jewelry](/arcanum/mana-storage) abandoned at a scene.

A practitioner who suppresses their mana output to avoid detection creates their own signature problem: the specific quality of active mana containment reads differently from a civilian with no well at all. Suppression announces itself to a trained sensing practitioner as surely as an unfamiliar signature does.

## The Governance Dimension

City-scale linked Mana Sensing — the Inquisition's pursuit tool — represents a concentration of surveillance capability that the Kingdom's [constitutional order](/governance/the-constitutional-order) addresses specifically. City-scale sensing operations require the same authorization level as a formal Inquisition operation: Commander of the Guard and Sovereign authorization, for a specific named pursuit target and a specific operational window. The tool does not exist as ambient city surveillance. It exists as a targeted pursuit instrument, deployed for specific operations and no others.

Whether that authorization framework is always honored depends on the Sovereign's character — which the Archive records as the correct answer for this Kingdom. The technology exists. The institutional constraint exists. The Sovereign's integrity is the check on whether the constraint is honored, and the [Inner Sanctum's](/governance/the-inner-sanctum) inclusion of senior senators provides the civilian governance dimension to deliberations that might otherwise lack it.

> Directorate note: a recruit once asked whether a large enough well makes Mana Submission unstoppable. No. It makes it expensive to stop. There is a difference, and the difference is the entire reason [the Imperial Command Council](/governance/the-imperial-command-council) reviews these deployments at all.
