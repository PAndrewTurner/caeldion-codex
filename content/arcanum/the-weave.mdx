*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. I: Fundamentals of the Weave. Distribution: general.*

Magic, in imperial doctrine, is not a gift and not a mystery. It is work — the conversion of a caster's internal energy through trained pattern into external effect. The practice is called weaving; the discipline of doing it without dying is called the Weave, and this Archive treats the two as one subject because the Directorate insists that they are.

## First Principles

The Weave obeys the same ledger as every other imperial undertaking: nothing is created, everything is paid for.

1. Effect is drawn from the caster's own reserve — the [mana well](/arcanum/mana-wells). There is no external source to borrow against; magic is channeled from within the body outward, never summoned from the atmosphere.
2. Conversion is never clean. Every working sheds waste heat into the caster's body — [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn), the single greatest killer of untrained casters.
3. Pattern is efficiency. A trained weave accomplishes with a cupful what an untrained surge spends a well on. The Academy teaches pattern before power, always.

## The Two Architectures: Script versus Control

The Directorate addresses the skill gap between trained specialists and newly conscripted commoners through two distinct casting architectures, and every soldier's kit is built around one or the other.

### Hard-Coded Scripts — the Equalizer

Issued to the lower ranks: the [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion), basic [Logistics Corps](/grand-army/logistics-corps) applications, [Engineering Corps](/grand-army/engineering-corps) support roles. The soldier provides the fuel; the weapon or equipment provides the spell. The formula is engraved into the item — a Legionnaire who has no idea how light is generated can still trigger a Flash Rune on their spear by pushing raw mana into the designated channel. The spear does the rest.

The trade is total: minimal training required, zero flexibility returned. The item does exactly one thing and cannot be made to do another.

### Variable Control — the Elite Standard

Issued in practice, if not in gear, to every soldier of the [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy), the [Azure Covenant](/grand-army/azure-covenant), the [Crimson Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard), the [Green Rangers](/grand-army/green-rangers), the [Sovereign's Arcanum](/grand-army/purple-guard), and the [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) without exception. The soldier modulates output directly through their own understanding of magical physics and biology — a Cobalt weaver can adjust the mana flow to their blade across a continuous spectrum, from a dim torch sufficient to navigate a corridor to an edge wreathed in enough heat to shear tempered steel.

The trade inverts: infinite adaptability, purchased with training, intelligence, and a well-managed reserve. A soldier who attempts Variable Control work their [well](/arcanum/mana-wells) cannot yet sustain is more dangerous to themselves than to the enemy.

## The Physics Application Doctrine

The Grand Army does not classify soldiers by element — fire, ice, void — but by Physics Application. Every soldier with a well has theoretical access to any type of magic; what differs between divisions is not *what* magic they can reach, but how doctrine trains them to apply it. ## The Weave Doctrine: A Ladder of Integration

The ultimate expression of Grand Army combat doctrine takes its name from the discipline itself: the seamless, simultaneous integration of physical combat and magical application. It is the standard expected of every upper-rank soldier and the aspiration of every recruit who earns their colors.

- **Amateur** — stops fighting to cast a spell.
- **Standard Army** — casts through the fight.
- **Weaver** — the spell is the follow-through of the physical strike, not an interruption of it. A blade ignites as it swings so the cut and the cauterization happen in the same motion.

### Hybrid Weaving in Practice

Casting conducted simultaneously with close combat was tactically impossible for most of recorded history: the Burn generated by combat-tempo casting exceeded what a moving, armored body could shed. It became doctrine in a single generation for one reason, and the Directorate names it plainly — [runic heat-sink armor](/arcanum/runic-armor).

By braiding multiple disciplines at once, a hybrid weaver reaches effects no single application produces alone:

- **Flight** — weaving mass negation with atmospheric magic renders the caster weightless, then uses wind to propel them off the ground and through the air, restoring weight on impact to strike from above.
- **Weather Generation** — combining thermal, atmospheric, and water magics to raise a hurricane, tornado, or thunderstorm.

High-level multi-disciplinary weaving demands intense concentration and a large mana expenditure. Casters who expect to work at this level habitually carry [stored reserves](/magic/mana-storage) in rings or similar objects rather than trust their well alone.

## Regulation

Weaving is a certified trade. Casters are rated by [well depth and pattern discipline](/arcanum/mana-wells) at the [Imperial College](/geography/caeldrion), and field authorization follows the rating, not the rank. An unrated caster weaving at combat tempo is, in the manual's words, "a casualty who has not finished the paperwork."
