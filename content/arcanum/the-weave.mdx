*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. I: Fundamentals of the Weave. Distribution: general.*

Magic, in imperial doctrine, is not a gift and not a mystery. It is work — the conversion of a caster's internal energy through trained pattern into external effect. The practice is called weaving; the discipline of doing it without dying is called the Weave, and this Archive treats the two as one subject because the Directorate insists that they are.

## First Principles

The Weave obeys the same ledger as every other imperial undertaking: nothing is created, everything is paid for.

1. Effect is drawn from the caster's own reserve — the [mana well](/arcanum/mana-wells). There is no external source to borrow against.
2. Conversion is never clean. Every working sheds waste heat into the caster's body — [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn), the single greatest killer of untrained casters.
3. Pattern is efficiency. A trained weave accomplishes with a cupful what an untrained surge spends a well on. The Academy teaches pattern before power, always.

## Hybrid Weaving

The advanced doctrine — casting conducted simultaneously with close combat — is the signature of the [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy). Hybrid Weaving was tactically impossible for most of recorded history: the Burn generated by combat-tempo casting exceeded what a moving, armored body could shed. It became doctrine in a single generation for one reason, and the Directorate names it plainly: [runic heat-sink armor](/arcanum/runic-armor).

## Regulation

Weaving is a certified trade. Casters are rated by well depth and pattern discipline at the [Imperial College](/geography/caeldrion), and field authorization follows the rating, not the rank. An unrated caster weaving at combat tempo is, in the manual's words, "a casualty who has not finished the paperwork."
