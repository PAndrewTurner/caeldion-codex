*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. III: The Burn. Distribution: general. Medical annexes restricted.*

No law of the [Weave](/arcanum/the-weave) is more absolute than this one: conversion is never clean. Every working sheds waste heat, and the heat sheds *inward* — into the caster's blood, nerves, and marrow. The Directorate's term is the Burn. Before the runic program, it killed more imperial casters than every enemy of the Empire combined.

## Presentation

Burn injury is graded in four degrees, and field medics are drilled to read them at a glance:

| Degree | Presentation | Field response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First | Flush, sweat, elevated pulse | Cease casting; hydrate; log the draw |
| Second | Tremor, skin mottling along the forearms | Casting bar, one full day; medic review |
| Third | Seizure, internal scalding, pattern collapse | Evacuate; Burn ward; survival probable |
| Fourth | — | Entry sealed by Medical Directorate order |

A caster at combat tempo without countermeasures reaches second degree in minutes. Pattern collapse at depth — see [mana wells](/arcanum/mana-wells) — dumps the well's remaining charge at once, and fourth-degree files are sealed for the same reason the Vanguard seals its worst breaches: the dead deserve better than curiosity.

## Countermeasures

The Empire's answer to the Burn is not restraint but engineering, pursued in layers:

1. **Pattern discipline** — the trained weave sheds a fraction of the surge's heat. Taught first, always.
2. **[Runic armor](/arcanum/runic-armor)** — silver channel-work that conducts the Burn out of the body and into the plate, where it belongs.
3. **[Arming doublets](/arcanum/arming-doublets)** — the treated under-layer that survives the plate doing its job.

Together these moved the Burn from the first line of the casualty ledger to the fourth. The Medical Directorate's Burn wards remain staffed at full establishment anyway. The Directorate does not explain this; veterans do not ask.

> Directorate note: the recruit's question "how much can I cast before it hurts" is officially answered "less than that." The manual regards the question itself as first-degree presentation.
