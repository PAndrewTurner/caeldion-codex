*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. III: The Burn. Distribution: general. Medical annexes restricted.*

No law of the [Weave](/arcanum/the-weave) is more absolute than this one: conversion is never clean. Every working sheds waste heat, and the heat sheds *inward* — into the caster's blood, nerves, and marrow. The Directorate's term is the Burn. Before the runic program, it killed more imperial casters than every enemy of the Empire combined.

## The Exertion Principle and the Four Tiers

Not all magical output costs equally. The physical toll scales directly with the magnitude of the effect produced, and the Directorate classifies every working into one of four output tiers, each with distinct physiological consequence.

| Tier | Class | Representative working | Physiological cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I | Ambient Output | A [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) Mender closing a surface wound; an [Iron Legion](/grand-army/iron-legion) trooper triggering a Flash Rune | Negligible heat; sustainable indefinitely, armored or not |
| II | Active Output | A [Green Ranger](/grand-army/green-rangers) guiding arrows across a prolonged engagement; a [Cobalt](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) weaver maintaining kinetic augmentation | Noticeable well expenditure; armor manages it for hours, but it accumulates |
| III | Intensive Output | A [Crimson Vanguard's](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) Thermal Dump; a Cobalt weaver's Blink | Substantial heat spike; executable in bursts, not chained without recovery |
| IV | Extreme Output | [Azure Covenant](/grand-army/azure-covenant) Coven Links; Arcanum Void applications; Cataclysmic Magic | The operational ceiling of human capacity; armor is the reason the caster survives their own spell, and no soldier sustains it twice without recovery |

## Presentation

Burn injury is graded in four stages, and field medics are drilled to read them at a glance:

| Stage | Presentation | Field response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 — The Flush | Body temperature rises beyond exertion levels, skin flushes red, capillaries dilate, performance degrades at the margins | Cease casting; hydrate; log the draw |
| 2 — The Fever | Capillaries burst, vision blurs and distorts, blood approaches dangerous temperature, cognitive function deteriorates | Casting bar, one full day; medic review; reaching this stage in the field is a command failure as much as a personal one |
| 3 — Mana Collapse | The nervous system begins to fail; body temperature spikes toward the survivable limit — "cooking from the inside out" in the soldiers' phrase | Evacuate; Burn ward; survival probable, though continued Stage 3 exposure degrades the armor's own ability to regulate the heat |
| 4 | — | Entry sealed by Medical Corps order |

A caster at combat tempo without countermeasures reaches Stage 2 in minutes. Pattern collapse at depth — see [mana wells](/arcanum/mana-wells) — dumps the well's remaining charge at once, and Stage 4 files are sealed for the same reason the [Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) seals its worst breaches: the dead deserve better than curiosity.

## The Fugue State

The one Burn presentation that defies every tier and every tier's countermeasures is the Fugue State — the involuntary, uncontrolled venting of a soldier's well under conditions of extreme stress, survival instinct, or psychological crisis. It is not a technique. It is not trainable. It is what happens when the body's deepest biological imperatives bypass every layer of learned control and access the well at its rawest possible level.

The State produces effects that match nothing in the soldier's training, divisional specialty, or established capability — the Archive's two standing examples remain instructive. Legionnaire Sylas Vane manifested Void Magic, [Arcanum](/grand-army/purple-guard)-tier, after three weeks in the Legion with no magical training whatsoever. Lady Elara vaporized a stone wall with a kinetic beam she had never been taught to produce. The Fugue State draws from a deeper place than training reaches, and there is no formal Army doctrine around it — it cannot be systematized because it cannot be reliably induced or controlled. The only institutional response is recognition: the Arcanum sentinel at the Black Crucible watches specifically for signs of Fugue State potential in recruits under stress, and the Army fast-tracks anyone who manifests it for immediate assessment.

In soldiers carrying [runic body inscription](/arcanum/runic-body-work), the Fugue State automatically activates every tattoo circuit at once, producing a striking full-body luminescence that maps the soldier's entire inscription architecture in visible light. The heat management benefit is partial — tattoos are less efficient than plate — but where armor may be unavailable, the automatic activation provides meaningful protection through the State's duration. Experienced observers recognize the display immediately as both extraordinary output and a loss of conscious control; the correct response is distance and containment, not engagement.

## Countermeasures

The Empire's answer to the Burn is not restraint but engineering, pursued in layers:

1. **Pattern discipline** — the trained weave sheds a fraction of the surge's heat. Taught first, always.
2. **[Runic armor](/arcanum/runic-armor)** — silver channel-work that conducts the Burn out of the body and into the plate, where it belongs.
3. **[Arming doublets](/arcanum/arming-doublets)** — the treated under-layer that survives the plate doing its job.
4. **[Runic body inscription](/arcanum/runic-body-work)** — the backup layer for soldiers caught without plate, less efficient but proven since long before the war-steel program existed.

Together these moved the Burn from the first line of the casualty ledger to the fourth. The [Medical Corps'](/grand-army/medical-corps) Burn wards remain staffed at full establishment anyway. The Directorate does not explain this; veterans do not ask.

> Directorate note: the recruit's question "how much can I cast before it hurts" is officially answered "less than that." The manual regards the question itself as first-stage presentation.
