*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. XII: Storage, Ambient Release, and Signature Forensics. Distribution: general. Forensic identification annexes restricted to the Medical Corps and Imperial High Court prosecutors of record.*

Every practitioner's [well](/arcanum/mana-wells) is not a static reservoir but a continuously renewing biological system. Old mana is displaced by new mana as part of the well's natural cycle, and the displaced mana is released from the body as an ambient field rather than being retained. This ambient release happens continuously regardless of whether the practitioner is actively using magic, whether their well is depleted or full, whether they are asleep or in combat. It is the biological byproduct of a living mana system, as natural and constant as breathing.

Mana Storage is the technique of capturing this continuous ambient release in a prepared external object rather than allowing it to disperse into the environment. The stored mana is the practitioner's own — it carries their specific biological signature, responds to their direction when drawn upon, and functions as an extension of their well rather than a foreign source. What would have been lost becomes a reserve.

## The Two Collection Methods

- **Passive ambient collection** — once trained, the practitioner maintains a background awareness of their ambient release and redirects it into a prepared storage item rather than allowing dispersal. This is a passive habit of magical consciousness, not an active technique — once established, it runs continuously without deliberate effort. The item fills gradually, over days and weeks, at the natural rate of the well's renewal cycle. A practitioner who wears a prepared storage ring every day accumulates a meaningful reserve without ever consciously spending well capacity.
- **Intentional active storage** — the practitioner deliberately pushes mana from their current well directly into the storage object. Faster than passive collection, filling the item in a single session rather than over days, but it costs the practitioner's current reserves directly and requires recovery time. Used when a full item is needed before a known engagement and passive collection cannot be waited out. Practitioners preparing for a major operation typically combine both: weeks of passive collection topped up with active filling immediately before deployment.

## Storage Objects — What Works and Why

Not every object holds stored mana equally. The object must either carry prepared runic inscription — creating the structural framework that stabilizes mana within the material rather than allowing dispersal — or be made of materials with high natural mana receptivity. Silver and gold, already the primary [runic inscription](/arcanum/runic-armor) substrates in the empire, have the highest natural receptivity of common materials. Specific crystalline gemstones have exceptional retention properties the Science Division has catalogued and incorporates into high-capacity storage items.

### Jewelry — The Standard Form

Rings, necklaces, and wristbands are the natural and preferred storage objects because they maintain continuous body contact. The ambient release radiates outward from the body, and a storage item worn against the skin intercepts that release at its source rather than trying to collect it at a distance: a ring over the finger through which most practitioners channel their output, a wristband over the primary channeling pathways of the wrist, a necklace near the chest where the well's primary biological location concentrates the release.

The empire's mineral wealth makes quality storage jewelry accessible at multiple price points. A retired Cobalt Maestro who wears a prepared gold ring every day builds their reserve continuously. A senior Ranger on extended field operations whose silver pendant has passively collected for weeks has a meaningful reserve without ever spending well capacity in preparation. The Science Division's senior Masterwork storage amulet — gem-set silver with precisely calibrated inscription — holds the largest capacity available outside [Purple Guard](/grand-army/purple-guard) equipment.

### Weapons — Functional With Complications

Storing mana in a sword, battle axe, or hammer works mechanically — the mana stabilizes in the weapon's material the same way it stabilizes in jewelry. The complication appears when the weapon is used for active magical channeling: the stored mana in the object shares a channel with the active flow, and the two streams interact. Since stored and channeled mana share the practitioner's biological signature, the interaction is not dangerous — but it produces unpredictability in the output the practitioner cannot fully control.

For practitioners requiring precision — Rangers, Medical Corps, Engineers performing structural repair — this unpredictability is a liability. For practitioners requiring maximum output rather than precision, weapon storage can be deliberately exploited: a [Crimson Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) who fills their hammer with stored mana the night before an assault through active intentional storage, with a full well recovered by morning, releases more on Thermal Dump impact than their well alone could produce. The interaction amplifies rather than disrupts. Senior Vanguard practitioners who understand this use weapon storage as a deliberate pre-assault technique — the trade of reduced precision for maximum force is, for the specific moment of a breach, often correct.

## Ambient Release — The Universal Signature

The ambient release produced by the well's renewal cycle is not merely the source material Mana Storage collects above. It is the fundamental mechanism by which [Mana Sensing](/magic/mana-manipulation) detects and identifies any individual with a well. Every person with a well — practitioner or not — produces continuous ambient release from the natural renewal cycle. A soldier whose well exists but who has never developed significant magical capability still produces ambient release. A civilian with a minimal well who has never trained a single application still has a signature. The ambient release carries the individual's specific mana signature just as their active output does — quieter, more diffuse, lower intensity, but always present and always identifiable.

### The Detection Spectrum

| Level | What it reveals |
| --- | --- |
| **Presence** | The most basic level — something with a well is here. Basic Mana Sensing registers this at the edge of its range with minimal training. |
| **Movement and Direction** | The ambient release shifts as the target moves through the sensing field, tracked in real time — the mechanism behind the Citadel sensing shift system, which detects continuous ambient presence rather than active magic use. |
| **Identity — Signature Matching** | Matching a specific ambient signature to a known individual. Citadel sensing Guards who have catalogued every regular occupant identify specific people by ambient release the way a person identifies a voice; Inquisition city-scale search filters ambient signatures across an entire population for one specific quality against the background of everyone else's. The target does nothing to produce it — the well's renewal cycle produces the signature continuously and involuntarily. |
| **State** | At high precision, the quality of ambient release communicates current condition. A practitioner actively channeling magic reads differently from one at rest; a recently depleted practitioner produces fainter release; stress and extreme exertion produce subtle changes an experienced sensing practitioner learns to read. |

### Non-Practitioners and Minimal Wells

People with no well at all produce no ambient signature — they are a person-shaped absence in the sensing field. In an environment where everyone around them has a well and produces continuous ambient release, genuine absence is itself detectable as an anomaly: a person with no well moving through the Citadel appears to a sensing Guard as a blank space where a signature should be, which is, in its own way, as notable as an unfamiliar signature. People with small or untrained wells produce faint ambient signatures — detectable at close range by a trained practitioner, but easily missed at distance.

### Signature Suppression — The Detection Problem

A practitioner who knows they are being tracked by Mana Sensing may attempt to suppress their ambient release — to contain the displacement the renewal cycle produces rather than allowing it to radiate outward. This is technically difficult and physiologically uncomfortable for any extended period: sustainable for minutes, increasingly uncomfortable over longer periods, physiologically inadvisable to sustain.

More critically, suppression produces its own detectable signature. Genuine absence — a person with no well — and active suppression — a practitioner containing their release — read differently to a trained sensing practitioner. The effort of suppression creates a subtle magical tension in the ambient field distinct from the clean absence of someone with no well at all. A Guard who encounters apparent absence in a location where someone should be has found something worth investigating rather than nothing.

### Storage Items and Signature Forensics

Because stored mana carries the practitioner's biological signature, storage items become forensic evidence. A storage ring found at a scene identifies its owner through the signature of the stored mana — the same way [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) Well Interference analysis identifies [drain](/magic/mana-draining) and [seal](/magic/well-sealing) signatures. The Medical Corps forensic function and the Mana Sensing identification function converge here: both provide practitioner identification from mana signature, from different evidence types. The Purple Guard's Inquisition function takes note of this specifically — a practitioner who abandons their storage jewelry at a scene has left something more identifying than a physical object. They have left a biological signature.

> Directorate note: recruits sometimes ask whether the Kingdom could build a ring that hides an ambient signature entirely. The honest answer is that the Directorate has tried. What comes back from the attempt is not silence — it is a very specific, very recognizable kind of noise.
