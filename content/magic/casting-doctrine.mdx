*Imperial War Academy, Combat Magic Curriculum, Lecture I: Foundations of Battle Casting. Distribution: all commissioned and field-promoted magical practitioners. This lecture opens every cadet's instruction in the Combat Magic sequence and is referenced by codexRef throughout the remainder of the curriculum.*

The Science Directorate's Arcanum Archive teaches what magic *is* — the biology of the well, the thermodynamics of the Burn, the engineering of the channel matrix. This curriculum teaches what a soldier *does* with it under fire. The distinction matters. A recruit who can recite [the Weave's](/arcanum/the-weave) first principles from memory and freezes at the first Stage 1 flush has learned the wrong half of the subject. This lecture, and the three that follow it, exist to close that gap.

## The Well in Combat

Every soldier who casts draws from the same organ: the [internal well](/arcanum/mana-wells), a biological reserve that behaves like trained musculature rather than a fuel gauge. In the classroom this is an abstraction. In a live engagement it is the single fact that determines whether a soldier is still fighting in ninety seconds or being carried off the field by their squad's [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) attachment.

Combat casting differs from garrison casting in one respect that the Academy drills relentlessly: the well does not announce its depletion politely. A caster who has trained only in controlled conditions — full concentration, no incoming fire, unlimited time to calibrate a working — discovers under combat pressure that adrenaline, fear, and the compressed decision window of an active engagement all draw on the same well the spell does. A soldier arriving at a fight already fatigued, already stressed, already three hours into a march, has less well to spend than the same soldier fresh from barracks, even though nothing about their trained capacity has changed. Field doctrine therefore rates every caster by *sustainable combat draw*, not by their peacetime maximum, and every officer is trained to ask "how much has this soldier already spent today" before assigning a casting-dependent task.

## Unarmored Output — The First Rule of the Breach

[Runic armor](/arcanum/runic-armor) is the reason Hybrid Weaving exists at all — the reason a soldier can cast at combat tempo without cooking from the inside out. Losing it mid-engagement is therefore not a cosmetic setback. It is a hard ceiling reimposed without warning.

| Output tier | Armored | Unarmored |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tier I | Free | Free |
| Tier II | Sustainable for hours | Manageable briefly; taxing for hours after |
| Tier III | Sustainable in disciplined bursts | One cast is the practical limit |
| Tier IV | Survivable only with full inscription | Unsurvivable outside extraordinary cases |

A cadet's first live-fire lesson in this principle is almost always secondhand — a case study, not a personal injury, because the Academy does not strip armor from trainees to prove a point. But every graduating officer can recite the operational consequence without hesitation: a [Cobalt](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) weaver who loses their gauntlets, a [Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard) whose runic weapon is knocked from their hand, has not lost their magic. They have lost their budget for spending it. See [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn) for the full physiological staging of what happens when that budget is exceeded anyway.

> Directorate note, reprinted in the cadet manual: "less than that" is the correct answer to every recruit's question about how much they can cast before it hurts. This curriculum does not soften the phrasing.

## The Exertion Principle

Not all output costs the same, and the four-tier scale that governs every combat-magic lecture in this curriculum exists because the cost curve is not linear. A soldier who treats Tier III output as a Tier I convenience is a soldier the Academy has failed to teach. The full tier breakdown — Ambient, Active, Intensive, Extreme — and its physiological staging belong to [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn); what this lecture adds is the tactical framing: output tier is a resource-allocation decision made under time pressure, not a menu selected at leisure. The soldier "still fighting when the engagement ends," in the Directorate's own phrasing, is never the one who spent every tier available at the first opportunity.

## The Fugue State as Combat Risk

Every other topic in this curriculum concerns a soldier's deliberate choices. The Fugue State concerns the moment deliberate choice stops mattering. It is the involuntary, uncontrolled venting of a soldier's well under extreme stress or survival pressure — not a technique, not trainable, and not something command doctrine can plan around beyond recognizing it after the fact. Legionnaire Sylas Vane manifesting Arcanum-tier Void Magic three weeks into Legion service is the Archive's standing proof that the State draws from somewhere training does not reach. See [the Burn](/arcanum/the-burn) for the full account and the automatic-tattoo-activation mechanic that accompanies it.

For the purposes of this curriculum, cadets are taught one operational rule and one only: a soldier displaying Fugue State symptoms — glassy focus, uncontrolled output, in tattooed soldiers a full-body luminescence with no deliberate casting behind it — is treated as a medical emergency in progress, not a combatant to engage tactically. Command doctrine is distance and containment. The [Sovereign's Arcanum](/grand-army/purple-guard) trains its own sentinels specifically to recognize the State's onset in recruits under stress, because a soldier who manifests it once is fast-tracked for assessment rather than punished for the disruption.

## Scripts versus Control — The Line That Divides the Army

Every soldier's relationship to combat magic is shaped by which of two casting architectures their kit is built around, and this division — more than rank, more than divisional color — is the true line between the common soldier and the elite caster.

- **Hard-coded scripts** put the spell in the equipment. The soldier supplies fuel; the item supplies the formula. This is the [Iron Legion's](/grand-army/iron-legion) standard issue and the baseline for most [Logistics Corps](/grand-army/logistics-corps) and [Engineering Corps](/grand-army/engineering-corps) support roles — minimal training required, and the item does exactly one thing regardless of who is holding it.
- **Variable Control** puts the spell in the soldier. Every practitioner of the [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy), the [Azure Covenant](/grand-army/azure-covenant), the [Crimson Vanguard](/grand-army/crimson-vanguard), the [Green Rangers](/grand-army/green-rangers), the [Sovereign's Arcanum](/grand-army/purple-guard), and the [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) casts this way without exception, modulating output across a continuous spectrum through their own understanding of the physics rather than a pre-engraved formula.

The trade is total in both directions. A scripted caster is combat-ready in a fraction of the time and cannot be made more dangerous than the rune allows. A Variable Control caster has, in principle, no ceiling on what a single working can achieve — and a soldier who reaches for output their well cannot yet sustain is a greater danger to themselves than to the enemy standing in front of them. Cross-division continuing education means the boundary is not permanent: a Legionnaire who develops the aptitude and puts in the years can train toward Variable Control capability, and the Academy's transfer-trial system exists in part to formalize exactly that migration.

## The Spectrum of Application

The Grand Army does not sort its casters by element. It sorts them by Physics Application — the doctrinal lens through which a division trains its soldiers to point the same underlying magic at different problems. Every soldier with a well has theoretical access to every application; what differs is emphasis, training, and the specific combat art a division has built around its signature use of the physics. The full taxonomy, division by division, is catalogued in [the Weave](/arcanum/the-weave); the combat expressions of that taxonomy — the named techniques a soldier actually executes under fire — are the subject of [Divisional Battle Arts](/magic/divisional-battle-arts), the third lecture in this sequence.

## Where This Curriculum Goes From Here

This lecture is the floor, not the ceiling. Three further subjects complete the Combat Magic sequence, each building on the foundation above:

- [Hybrid Weaving](/magic/hybrid-weaving) — the Maestro art of braiding steel and spell, and spell with spell, into a single continuous motion.
- [The Coven Link](/magic/the-coven-link) — linked casting as a weapon system, from the individual Link to coordinated multi-Coven bombardment doctrine.
- [Divisional Battle Arts](/magic/divisional-battle-arts) — the named, signature combat techniques each division has built on the Physics Application it emphasizes.

Cadets who additionally require the counter-magic curriculum — the rare sub-specialties that attack a caster's well directly rather than projecting force at a target — are directed to [Well Sealing](/magic/well-sealing), [Mana Draining](/magic/mana-draining), [Mana Manipulation](/magic/mana-manipulation), and [Mana Storage](/magic/mana-storage), taught separately at the Arcanum's discretion given their sensitivity.

> Directorate note: recruits who ask which of these four lectures matters most are told the truth — the one that describes the fight they are about to be in. All four matter. Only one applies today.
