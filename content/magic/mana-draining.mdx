*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. X: Draining and Drain Countermeasures. Distribution: restricted. Full annex access limited to the Arcanum, the Medical Corps' Well Interference sub-specialty, and Imperial High Court prosecutors of record.*

Where [well sealing](/magic/well-sealing) closes the vault door, mana draining reaches inside and takes what is there. The practitioner extends their own mana externally, establishes a siphon connection with the target's [well](/arcanum/mana-wells), and pulls the target's mana out of their body and into their own system. The siphoned mana temporarily augments the drainer's well. The victim's well is depleted — ranging from partial depletion to permanent channel damage to death, depending on the depth and duration of the drain.

Because the well is a biological organ — established throughout this Archive as something closer to a muscle than a fuel tank — mana draining is not simply the taking of a resource. It is a biological assault on a living system. The comparison to physical assault understates the injury: a drained practitioner has had something taken from them at a level more fundamental than any external wound reaches.

## Why It Is Rare

Mana draining requires two demanding capabilities in precise combination that most mages never develop simultaneously.

- **Reverse current control** — perfect internal control of one's own mana, sufficient to create a pull rather than a push. Every standard magical technique, from [the Weave's](/arcanum/the-weave) simplest working to [Cataclysmic Magic](/grand-army/purple-guard), involves projecting mana outward. Draining requires inverting that principle entirely — a level of internal precision most high-level practitioners never develop, because they have no reason to.
- **External manipulation precision** — the fine-work standard the Medical Corps' Well Interference sub-specialty requires, applied offensively and without the Medical Corps' training in biological architecture. An imprecise drainer does not simply fail to drain. They risk damaging the target's flow channels catastrophically, turning an intended partial drain into accidental permanent severance.

Practitioners who develop exceptional internal control typically do so in combat traditions where outward projection is everything. Practitioners who develop external precision typically work in [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) or Aegis Ward contexts, where that precision is applied carefully and therapeutically. The mana drainer is someone who developed both capabilities — and then trained specifically to combine them in their most demanding and most dangerous application. The combination is genuinely rare across the world's magical traditions, non-universal and otherwise.

## The Three Severity Levels

| Level | Mechanism | Consequence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Partial Drain** | Well depleted below the target's functional threshold | Cannot cast; runic armor goes dark as Passive Ignition loses its mana supply. Recovery over time, the way a muscle recovers from exertion. No permanent damage if the drain stops before going deeper. |
| **Deep Drain** | Mana pulled past the natural depletion threshold, forcing the well to surrender reserves retained for baseline biological function | Damage begins to become permanent — comparable to the well damage catastrophic self-overuse produces, but inflicted from outside. Medical Corps Well Interference treatment required. Some deep drain damage is irreparable. |
| **Complete Drain** | The well stripped entirely | Potentially fatal — a biological trauma equivalent to massive blood loss. Emergency Medical Corps response required; survival is not guaranteed, and permanent capability loss is almost certain in survivors. |

## Legal Status — The Combat Exception

Mana draining is legal on the battlefield against enemy combatants and illegal in almost every other circumstance. The exception follows the same logic as every other combat exception recognized by the Kingdom's [constitutional order](/governance/the-constitutional-order): on a battlefield, the enemy is trying to kill you, and the tools available to prevent that are not held to the ethical constraints that govern civilian conduct.

A battlefield drain achieves the combat objective — neutralizing an enemy magical practitioner — without necessarily requiring their death. A drained mage who survives and is captured is a prisoner. A drained mage who retreats is no longer a threat. The drain is, in some respects, a more humane combat technique than killing, which is part of why the exception exists.

The battlefield application is rarer even than the civilian technique. A combat target is moving, defending, actively using their well, and potentially wearing runic armor that disrupts the fine magical work the drain requires, all while surrounded by other magical applications generating interference. The window for a successful battlefield drain is narrow. Practitioners who can execute it reliably under combat conditions are extraordinarily rare — perhaps a handful in any generation, across the entire world.

## Civilian Prohibition and Sentencing

Using mana draining against any non-combatant, in any non-battlefield context, is a grave crime punishable by imprisonment in Fortress Imperium. The Prisoner Seal that contains a convicted drainer's magical capability is specifically fitted to the crime — it subjects them to a permanent version of what they inflicted on others. They cannot access their well. They cannot drain anyone. Their capability is contained behind the same architecture that holds the empire's most dangerous prisoners.

| Circumstance | Sentence |
| --- | --- |
| Single incident, no permanent harm | Fortress Imperium — a defined term. Serious. Life-altering. |
| Repeated incidents, or partial permanent harm | Fortress Imperium — extended term, potentially decades. |
| Deep drain causing permanent capability loss | Fortress Imperium — lifetime imprisonment. |
| Complete drain causing death | Fortress Imperium lifetime, or the empire's Capitol equivalent, depending on circumstance. |

> Directorate note: recruits sometimes ask whether the combat exception can be stretched to cover a "nearly civilian" encounter. It cannot. The Imperial High Court has never once found for a defendant who argued the line was blurry.

## Medical Corps Forensic Role

Well Interference specialists distinguish between voluntary depletion, traumatic external drain, and permanent channel damage from drain. The biological signature of an external drain is distinguishable from self-inflicted depletion through specific patterns in the flow channels — evidence of mana being pulled rather than flowing outward normally. [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) testimony is central to mana drain criminal prosecution: a Senior Physician or Well Interference specialist who examines the victim and testifies to the specific biological signature of an external drain is the prosecution's most important witness. Where the drainer left a recoverable trace, the [mana signature](/magic/mana-manipulation) analysis that identifies sealers from a sealed well applies equally to identifying drainers from a drained one.

## Strategic Implications

A confirmed battlefield drainer in an enemy force is among the highest-priority intelligence targets the Grand Army identifies — higher than most individual enemy soldiers, comparable to a confirmed [well sealer](/magic/well-sealing). A drainer who reaches a [Sovereign's Arcanum](/grand-army/purple-guard) member's well in combat does not merely incapacitate the Guard. They potentially acquire temporary access to the largest magical reservoir any human possesses. For the duration the drained mana remains in the drainer's system — minutes to hours before it dissipates or is used — they hold power that was never theirs, while the Guard is incapacitated. Both outcomes are catastrophic and simultaneous.

[The Eye](/governance/the-eye) tracks confirmed drainers in foreign forces alongside well sealers as a primary intelligence priority, and the same Purple Guard detection training that identifies a sealing attempt is trained to identify a draining attempt — the two are different in mechanism but both involve hostile external manipulation of an ally's well. Confirmed intelligence of an enemy force fielding trained drainers triggers immediate Inquisition review, coordinated at the level of the [Imperial Command Council](/governance/the-imperial-command-council).

> Directorate note: the honest answer to "how do I defend against a drainer" is the same answer the Directorate gives for sealing — build the awareness to recognize the attempt before it completes. There is no armor plate for this one.
