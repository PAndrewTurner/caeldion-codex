*Extracted from the Science Directorate Field Manual, Vol. IX: Sealing and Sealed Countermeasures. Distribution: general. Sealer identification annexes restricted to the Arcanum.*

Among the most dangerous and rarest magical sub-specialties in existence is well sealing — the projection of a concentrated magical obstruction onto a target's [mana well](/arcanum/mana-wells), blocking their access to it entirely. A sealed mage cannot cast. Cannot activate runic gear. Cannot access any magical capability until the seal is removed. Well sealing is not a technique that emerges from general training; it is a deep, specific discipline requiring years of dedicated development, genuinely rare even among the most capable mages in the world.

## Two Kinds of Sophistication

The relationship between a combat well seal and the [Sigil](/arcanum/runic-body-work) placed on every [Sovereign's Arcanum](/grand-army/purple-guard) member illuminates what makes each formidable in its own, entirely different way.

The Sigil is the pinnacle of what the Directorate's inscription tradition can produce — custom-calibrated to a specific Guard's well architecture through extended study, unbreakable by the wearer, and designed to function under any condition: combat, unconsciousness, Fugue State. Its sophistication is the sophistication of a vault door engineered by the world's greatest locksmith for one specific lock. It resists removal except through sanctioned channels, and no improvised force removes it cleanly.

A combat well seal is formidable for the opposite reason. It is dynamic — projected in real time against a resisting, unfamiliar target, at combat speed, in conditions where a mistake means death. No prior study of the target's architecture. No inscription. No preparation. Its sophistication is the sophistication of a master safecracker working blind and under fire, who opens a lock they never designed. It lacks the Sigil's permanence — a combat seal can be broken with sufficient force or skill in ways the Sigil cannot — but it can be deployed against anyone, anywhere, without warning.

Both are genuine peaks of entirely different disciplines, and an enemy fielding a trained sealer has found a real counter to the Arcanum — not the same counter as breaking a Sigil, and it does not need to be. Trained well sealers are extraordinarily rare; a confirmed enemy sealer is a priority intelligence target for the Arcanum's Inquisition function.

## The Sealing Mechanism

The sealing mage projects a concentrated burst of sealing magic onto the target's well access pathway, creating an obstruction that blocks the well from being reached. The seal does not drain or destroy the reservoir — the magic remains intact beneath the obstruction; the target simply cannot reach it. Once seated, the seal persists without the sealer maintaining concentration, freeing them to disengage, reposition, or address other threats immediately.

A vigilant mage with sufficient awareness can detect and interrupt a sealing attempt before it completes — the projection has a recognizable signature to those trained to identify it. This creates a narrow window of vulnerability for the sealer: the attempt requires surprise, restraint of the target, or overwhelming speed of execution to succeed.

## Breaking the Seal — Two Methods

- **Blunt Magical Force** — fast, available to any mage with sufficient well output. Shatters the obstruction directly, restoring access immediately. The risk is calibration: insufficient force fails to break the seal; excessive force propagates concussive damage into the ally's well and body, analogous to internal Burn injury. The correct method when the sealed ally is in immediate danger and time is not available.
- **Surgical Precision** — slower, requiring Variable Control mastery at [Medical Corps](/grand-army/medical-corps) specialist level. Dismantles the obstruction carefully rather than shattering it, with no risk to the sealed mage's well or body and full capability restored without residual damage. In field conditions this is typically performed by the sealed mage's partner, if sufficiently trained, or by Medical Corps personnel in post-engagement recovery.

## Strategic Implications

The vulnerability applies regardless of power level. A [Sovereign's Arcanum](/grand-army/purple-guard) member with an exceptionally large well is harder to seal — the obstruction required is proportionally more substantial — but not immune. A trained sealer who successfully seals a Guard's well leaves the most dangerous individual in the Army physically capable but magically inert: still the most accomplished unarmed combatant in most rooms, but without Void applications, Cataclysmic Magic, or any tier output above unassisted physical capability.

The same vulnerability reaches [Cobalt Ascendancy](/grand-army/cobalt-ascendancy) weavers. A sealed weaver retains their physical training, their equipment, and their tactical judgment — but loses the Blink, the concussive mid-parry burst, and the kinetic augmentation that defines their combat ceiling. In a Ranger-style deployment, operating without a partner capable of breaking a seal, this is a genuine operational consideration.

Non-universal rune systems compound the threat: an enemy tradition's sealing magic may present a signature the Army's practitioners do not recognize until the seal is already in place. Understanding what foreign sealing doctrine looks like before Grand Army assets encounter it in the field is a standing priority for [The Eye](/governance/the-eye), whose ongoing tracking of the well sealer proliferation problem is detailed in its own entry. Confirmed intelligence of an enemy force fielding trained sealers triggers immediate Inquisition response, coordinated at the level of the [Imperial Command Council](/governance/the-imperial-command-council).

> Directorate note: recruits ask whether a large well is a defense against sealing. The correct answer is "a taller wall, not a different wall." Build technique, not just depth.
